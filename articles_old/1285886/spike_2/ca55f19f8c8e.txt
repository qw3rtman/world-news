He added that he is unhappy with the pace and substance of negotiations, saying he does not believe Iran is prepared to offer enough concessions and that discussions will continue through the day.

Iran and the U.S. ended a long day of indirect nuclear negotiations in Geneva yesterday without a breakthrough, prolonging fears that the standoff could tip the Middle East into another war. Despite signs of progress from Omani mediators, Tehran reaffirmed its refusal to halt uranium enrichment or ship material abroad, while Washington continues to demand deeper concessions as American military forces mass across the region.

The carrier departed Crete on February 26 after a resupply stop and moved through the Strait of Gibraltar toward the eastern Mediterranean, part of a broader deployment ordered by President Donald Trump as he weighs potential military action.

President Donald Trump wants Iran to halt enrichment entirely and address missiles and regional proxies—issues Tehran refuses to negotiate.
Iranian state TV reported Tehran will continue enriching uranium, reject transferring stockpiles abroad, and seek sanctions relief.
Oman’s foreign minister said there was “significant progress,” though neither side detailed what that meant.

The talks marked the third attempt since last year’s brief war between Israel and Iran to revive diplomacy around Tehran’s nuclear program. Despite hours of discussion, the two sides remain divided on fundamental issues: the future of uranium enrichment, sanctions relief, and whether Iran must address its missile arsenal and regional proxies.

Iran insists its nuclear program is peaceful and that enrichment is a sovereign right. The U.S. argues that without strict limits and inspections, Iran could rapidly rebuild capabilities damaged in last year’s strikes. Satellite imagery has already shown activity at bombed sites, fueling suspicions in Washington.

Oman’s mediation kept both delegations at the table longer than expected, but diplomats left Geneva acknowledging that major gaps remain.
