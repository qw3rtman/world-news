President Donald Trump said he has not yet made a final decision on whether to take military action against Iran, telling a reporter, “No, I haven’t,” when asked directly about his plans. He added that he is unhappy with the pace and substance of negotiations, saying he does not believe Iran is prepared to offer enough concessions and that discussions will continue through the day.

Iran and the U.S. ended a long day of indirect nuclear negotiations in Geneva yesterday without a breakthrough, prolonging fears that the standoff could tip the Middle East into another war. Despite signs of progress from Omani mediators, Tehran reaffirmed its refusal to halt uranium enrichment or ship material abroad, while Washington continues to demand deeper concessions as American military forces mass across the region.

The USS Gerald R. Ford, the world’s largest aircraft carrier, has arrived off Israel’s coast as the United States accelerates its military buildup amid rising tensions with Iran. The carrier departed Crete on February 26 after a resupply stop and moved through the Strait of Gibraltar toward the eastern Mediterranean, part of a broader deployment ordered by President Donald Trump as he weighs potential military action.
