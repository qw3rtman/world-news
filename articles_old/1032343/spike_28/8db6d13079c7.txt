09:07 (IST) Mar 06

Gold, Silver Price Today: Central bank gold buying momentum eases

Central banks recorded net gold purchases of 5 tonnes in January, marking a sharp decline from the average monthly buying pace of 27 tonnes seen throughout 2025. This drop represents a decrease of more than 80 per cent compared with the rate of purchases during the previous year.“Central bank gold buying momentum eased at the start of the year, compared to the prior 12 month average of 27t,” wrote Marissa Salim, Senior Research Lead for Asia Pacific at the World Gold Council. She said fluctuating gold prices and seasonal influences may have led some institutions to moderate their buying activity early in the year. The opening months of the calendar year typically coincide with a quieter phase for several central banks, which can temporarily influence their purchasing trends.

Gold, Silver Price Today: Central bank gold buying down

Gold’s strong upward momentum in recent years has been driven by multiple factors, including geopolitical uncertainties, steady investor inflows and continued purchases by central banks. Among these, buying by the official sector has served as one of the most important structural supports for gold prices since 2022. However, fresh data released by the World Gold Council two days ago indicates that this support showed signs of weakening at the beginning of 2026, at least for the time being.According to the council’s latest monthly report, central bank demand for gold dropped significantly in January compared with the pace seen during the previous year. Analysts note that the decline is likely due to short term influences rather than a lasting change in strategy, but the figures nevertheless represent a noticeable break after a prolonged phase of consistent accumulation by central banks.

Gold, Silver Price Today: Gold price surge prompts Ghana's royalty scale proposal

China, the United States and several Western governments have jointly urged Ghana to reconsider plans to raise gold royalties, warning that the move could negatively affect some of the world’s largest mining companies. The unusual diplomatic coordination emerged through discussions with Ghanaian authorities, according to three sources familiar with the matter as well as a letter issued by an industry association.Ghana, Africa’s leading gold producer, is proposing to replace its current fixed royalty rate of 5 percent with a variable structure that would range from 5 percent to 12 percent, depending on prevailing gold prices. The proposed change forms part of the country’s broader strategy to increase revenue from the precious metal as bullion prices continue to reach successive record highs.
