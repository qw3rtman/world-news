After Israel's ultimatum, Lebanon orders arrest of Iran IRGC members

After threatening to strike Iran’s military sites in Lebanon if it did not halt attacks in the past 72 hours, Israel has sparked a government reaction from Lebanon, which ordered the arrest of members of Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC).

On Saturday, the cabinet issued a statement ordering the detention of members of the IRGC, including the Iran-backed Lebanese militant group Hezbollah, for “acts of sabotage and penetration.” The statement, which attributed the decision to a “crisis committee” formed by the government under Article 32 of the constitution, did not elaborate further. The article 32 committee is responsible for overseeing national security matters and coordinating the defense of the country.

Iran’s Supreme National Security Council also condemned the Israeli threats as “acts of aggression against Iran’s national and military dignity and sovereignty.” It reiterated that Tehran would spare no effort to counter threats against its interests and allies.

Hezbollah, which holds significant influence in Lebanon and is a major political and military force, has a long-standing alliance with Iran. The IRGC has long been a target of Israeli intelligence and military operations. Israel has repeatedly accused the IRGC of escalating tensions in the region by supporting its proxies, including Hezbollah.

The Lebanese government's move comes at a time of heightened military and political tensions in the region. Since the outbreak of war in Gaza, Israeli military strikes have escalated against targets in Lebanon, including Hezbollah posts and IRGC operatives. The country has seen rising casualties and damage, with attacks reported in mostly southern Lebanon, near the Israeli border.

Israel’s latest ultimatum is a renewed warning to Iran and its allies in the region. It has also raised concerns about the potential for a wider regional conflict involving other Iranian-aligned groups and Israeli military actions.

Lebanon’s decision to arrest IRGC members is a significant legal and political development, even though its enforcement and implications will need further analysis. It reflects a rare attempt by the Lebanese government to act independently in response to international pressure and threats, particularly with regard to Iran’s presence and influence in the country.
