Public Storage (PSA.N) has agreed to acquire smaller rival National Storage Affiliates (NSA.N) in an all-stock deal valued at about $10.5 billion including debt, the self-storage facilities operator said on Monday, as it expands in high-growth regions in the United States.

Shareholders of National Storage Affiliates will receive 0.14 shares of Public Storage common stock for each share they own, valuing the deal at about $41.68 per share.

Shares of National were up 27% in afternoon trading, while Public Storage fell 3.9%.

The deal will expand Public Storage's presence in high-growth regions such as the Sun Belt and is expected to generate $110 million to $130 million in annual synergies, while boosting funds from operations per share after closing.

Analysts at BMO Capital Markets said Public Storage's larger scale and brand could help improve the performance of National Storage Affiliates' properties, which have lagged peers.

They also said the company may push rents more aggressively and cut costs, particularly by reducing "in-store labor" and administrative expenses.

Public Storage said the boards of trustees of both the companies have unanimously approved the deal, which is expected to close in the third quarter of 2026.

Ahead of the deal's closing, Public Storage and partners of National Storage Affiliates will form a joint venture valued at roughly $3.3 billion.

National Storage's operating partnership unitholders are expected to own about 80% of the venture, while Public Storage will hold the remaining stake and manage the portfolio.

Public Storage also said it will repay National Storage's bank debt and senior notes while assuming certain existing mortgage debt and preferred equity. To finance the transaction, the company has secured $4 billion in committed financing from Goldman Sachs and Wells Fargo.

Colorado-based National Storage Affiliates owns a portfolio of more than 1,000 self-storage properties, offering about 69 million rentable square feet across roughly 550,000 units in 37 U.S. states and Puerto Rico.
