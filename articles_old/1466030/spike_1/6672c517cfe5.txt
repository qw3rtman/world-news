Names of four US soldiers killed in Iran released

Four U.S. soldiers killed in the Iran war were identified Sunday by the Pentagon, according to reports from multiple outlets.

The men, all part of a security team assigned to protect a base in Erbil, Iraq, died Monday when their infantry fighting vehicle was hit during an Iranian strike near Mehran, western Iran, the Department of Defense said. Final names are yet to be released.

More than 50,000 U.S. troops have been deployed to the Middle East in the past weeks following the escalation of hostilities between the U.S. and Iran.

The U.S. responded to Iranian aggression with airstrikes in which multiple Iranian missile sites, command and control positions and radar systems were destroyed, military officials said.
