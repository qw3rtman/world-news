James Talarico claimed the Democratic nomination for U.S. Senate in Texas on Tuesday, defeating Congresswoman Jasmine Crockett after a hard-fought primary defined by record spending and competing visions for the party’s future.

The Associated Press called the race for State Representative Talarico at 7:37 a.m. on Wednesday.

His general election opponent will be decided on a May runoff between longtime Republican Senator John Cornyn and MAGA favorite Ken Paxton.

## Why It Matters

The Talarico-Crockett contest marked the first high-profile Democratic primary of the 2026 cycle, and its outcome, along with the upcoming Republican primary, will help determine whether Democrats have a credible path to reclaiming the Senate.

Democrats have not won a U.S. Senate race in Texas since 1988, making their 2026 nominee a clear underdog in November. Still, party leaders say changing suburban demographics and potential Republican divisions could make the race more competitive in a midterm year.

## What To Know

The primary became a high-profile contest that drew national attention, fueled by record fundraising and unprecedented advertising spending in a Texas Senate race. In the final days before voting, public polling showed a tight race.

An Emerson College Polling survey conducted February 26–27 showed Talarico leading 52 percent to Crockett’s 47 percent, within the margin of error. A University of Texas/Texas Politics Project poll conducted earlier in February showed Crockett ahead 56 percent to 44 percent.

Advertising and reserved ad time topped $110 million ahead of Tuesday’s vote—the most ever for a Texas Senate primary, according to data cited by the Associated Press.

Talarico, a Texas state lawmaker first elected in 2018 and viewed as a rising Democrat focused on economic populism and broad voter appeal, reported raising more than $21 million through last week. His campaign said it brought in $2.5 million in the 24 hours after late-night host Stephen Colbert canceled a planned interview, a move that drew national attention to the race.

Crockett, elected to Congress in 2022 to represent a Dallas-area district, raised nearly $8.6 million. Much of that came from funds transferred from her House campaign after she entered the Senate race in December. In the final days before the primary, she secured an endorsement from former Vice President Kamala Harris.

## What People Are Saying

State Representative James Talarico previously told Newsweek: "I started off this campaign by saying I don't see politics as left versus right, I see it as top versus bottom. And I see the billionaires who are trying to divide us with their social media algorithms, with their cable news networks, with the politicians who are fighting on our screens.

U.S. Representative Jasmine Crockett previously told Newsweek: "We are living in unprecedented times, and so I think an unprecedented candidate is exactly what it's going to take to actually be able to flip this seat. Right now, the Republicans have been losing all over this country, and even when they're not losing, well, those margins are shrinking. But more importantly, I think that our base is hungry for something different."

## What Happens Next

The Democratic nominee will now advance to the November general election, where Republicans remain favored in a state that has not elected a Democratic U.S. senator since 1988. Texas Democrats have not won statewide office in Texas, from governor to railroad commissioner, since 1994.
