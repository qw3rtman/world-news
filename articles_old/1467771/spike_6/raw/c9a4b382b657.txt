# The Worst Case Scenario for Oil Is Here. $100 Crude Could Be Coming.

By Avi Salzman

The conflict between the U.S. and Iran could cause oil prices to spike. (Middle East Images/AFP via Getty Images)

The war in Iran has led to a worst-case scenario for the oil market: the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, through which 20% of the world’s oil flows. A prolonged closure could send international oil prices above $100 a barrel, a level they haven’t hit since 2022.
