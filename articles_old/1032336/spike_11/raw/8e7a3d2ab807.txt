# Gold and silver prices soar: Yellow metal hits Rs 1.66 lakh, silver gains Rs 20,000 — should you buy on dips?

Gold and silver metals inched higher on Monday, on the back of strong safe-haven demand from investors amid ongoing geopolitical tensions intensifying in the Middle East. The yellow metal rose by Rs 7,000 per 10 grams to Rs 1.66 lakh while silver inched to Rs 2.86 lakh, recording a gain of Rs 20,000 per kg. "If the West Asian conflict continues, the risk premium for investors would increase, pushing gold prices in India to new records," Aksha Kamboj, vice president at India Bullion & Jewellers Association (IBJA) told ET. The expert added, "Gold has traditionally been a safe-haven asset for investors, and the sustained demand for gold could boost prices to new highs." The rise in gold and silver prices are accompanied by growing concerns over crude oil. Since the joint Israeli and US military strikes on Iran on February 28, followed by Iran’s retaliatory actions against Gulf nations, crude oil prices have climbed, creating added pressure for India, which is heavily reliant on Middle Eastern oil imports.

Since January, gold has already gained 24%, while silver has surged 30%.
