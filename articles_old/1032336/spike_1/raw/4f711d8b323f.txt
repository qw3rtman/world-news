# Gold, Silver Rate Today Live Updates: Gold, silver prices down after seeing biggest rise in 3 weeks

Gold Silver Rate Today Live Updates: The ongoing geopolitical and economic developments have put focus back on gold and silver prices this week. The US Supreme Court has ruled that the reciprocal tariffs imposed by President Donald Trump are illegal. In anger, Trump has imposed a flat 15% global tariff on all trading partners. At the same time, uncertainty on the Iran-US talks front is fueling safe haven demand. Which way are gold and silver prices headed in the near-term? What should investors know about the long-term target for gold and silver prices, and will they beat stock markets in 2026 as well? Track TOI’s live coverage to check the latest on gold and silver prices today:

15:57 (IST) Feb 24

Gold Rate Today Live: Strong central bank buying in last few quarters

Official-sector demand now represents a structural pillar rather than a tactical response. Central banks continued to assert themselves as the most consistent structural buyers in the gold market through 2025 and into early 2026.Central banks collectively net purchased around 1,000 tons per year for four consecutive years—many emerging market and middle-income economies have pushed to diversify reserves further in response to geopolitical and fiscal pressures. Nations such as Poland, Kazakhstan, Brazil, and India expanded holdings significantly, while substantial, but opaque accumulation in China and Russia continued through domestic channels, says Motilal Oswal Financial Services in its latest report.

Gold Rate Today Live: Why gold is being sold

“There was a degree of selling by investors locking in gains after prices surged to roughly $5,249 per ounce,” said Zain Vawda, an analyst at MarketPulse by OANDA. “Another likely influence was the Trump administration’s announcement of a fresh tariff, which offered some short-term visibility regarding trade policy.”Gold, widely regarded as a safe-haven investment, typically attracts demand during periods marked by economic or geopolitical instability.

Silver Price Today Live: Gold, Silver ETFs drop

Gold and silver exchange-traded funds declined by as much as 3% on Tuesday after gold and silver futures on the MCX opened slightly weaker, as investors balanced the impact of a firmer US dollar against ongoing uncertainty over US tariff policies and escalating tensions between Washington and Tehran.Among gold-based funds, Groww Gold ETF, SBI Gold ETF and HDFC Gold ETF each dropped about 3%. 07:30 (IST) Feb 24

Gold Rate Today Live: FM Sitharaman on gold price rise

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman on Monday said that the recent surge in gold prices is largely driven by substantial buying by central banks around the world. She added that both the government and the Reserve Bank of India are closely monitoring gold imports, although current levels are not considered “alarming.”Between April and December last year, India’s gold imports, measured in value terms, rose by roughly USD 1 billion year-on-year to reach about USD 50 billion.RBI Governor Sanjay Malhotra noted that January witnessed a sharp jump in both the value and volume of gold imports, and said the central bank is examining the data in detail.Sitharaman pointed out that gold has traditionally been a preferred form of investment for Indian households, whether held as an asset or in the form of jewellery. Since domestic demand exceeds supply, India remains a net importer of the precious metal.She said the situation is under observation, but emphasized that given the long-standing inclination in India toward purchasing and investing in gold, imports have not crossed any concerning threshold. Nonetheless, she reiterated that the RBI will continue to keep a close watch on the trend.

05:30 (IST) Feb 24

Gold Rate Today Live: Fresh concerns over tariffs fuel rise

Nonetheless, he noted that talks between the parties are set to resume in Geneva on Thursday.

Gold Rate Today Live: Rebuilding of the geopolitical risk premium

"Gold’s renewed upswing after a healthy correction reflects a rebuilding of the geopolitical risk premium. Escalating US-Iran tensions and fresh uncertainty around US trade policy, mainly driven by the Supreme Court’s 6–3 ruling on Trump’s tariff framework and the president’s push for a new global levy, are pushing investors back toward safe havens. In this backdrop, gold is once again acting as insurance against tail risks in geopolitics and trade. From a credit rating perspective, the transmission is clearest in export-oriented manufacturers, shipping and logistics, and sectors like retail and pharma, where volatility in tariffs and currencies can squeeze margins. Banks and NBFCs with concentrated exposure to these segments may face modest asset‑quality pressures if volatility persists, while gold‑backed products should remain relatively resilient as long as the underlying collateral quality stays intact,” says Rajeev Sharan, Head – Criteria, Model Development & Research, Brickwork Ratings.
