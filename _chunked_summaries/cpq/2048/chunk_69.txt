### Ken Paxton, John Cornyn Head to Runoff in Texas GOP Senate Primary - Newsweek | 2026-03-04
Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton and U.S. Senator John Cornyn are headed to a runoff in the Texas Republican U.S. Senate primary, the Associated Press projected, after neither candidate secured a majority of the vote Tuesday; under Texas law a candidate must receive more than 50 percent to avoid a runoff, so they will lock horns in a May runoff to decide the Republican nominee. The race was a test of the Republican Party’s direction in Texas, where the GOP has long dominated and Texas has not elected a Democrat to statewide federal office in more than three decades, though Democrats have narrowed margins in major metropolitan areas in recent cycles. The primary followed months of heavy spending and sharp attacks, with the candidates and allied super PACs spending tens of millions of dollars on television, digital ads and mail, making it one of the most expensive Republican primaries in Texas in recent years; public polling in the final days showed a narrow edge for Paxton but a competitive race overall, with a RealClearPolling average at Paxton 37.3 percent and Cornyn 35 percent and an Emerson College survey listing Paxton ahead by 4 points, while neither candidate consistently polled above 40 percent in the three-way field that also included U.S. Representative Wesley Hunt. Prediction markets showed stronger confidence in Paxton, with Polymarket pricing him at 82 percent to win versus 17 percent for Cornyn and Kalshi showing Paxton’s “Yes” shares at 82 cents and Cornyn’s at 18 cents. Paxton and outside groups supporting him invested heavily in advertising promoting his record as attorney general and his alignment with President Donald Trump, while a pro-Paxton super PAC organized additional campaign stops ahead of early voting; Cornyn relied on his long Senate tenure and backing from Republican leadership-aligned groups, which spent more than $50 million on advertising, much of it contrasting his experience with Paxton’s 2023 impeachment. Cornyn’s biography includes service on the state Supreme Court, as state attorney general before his election to the Senate in 2002, and as Senate Republican whip, and he lost a bid for the top Senate leadership position in 2024 to Sen. John Thune of South Dakota. Paxton and Hunt both attacked Cornyn’s bipartisanship during the Biden administration.
