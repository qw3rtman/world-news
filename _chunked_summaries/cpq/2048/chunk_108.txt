Powell said the Fed would need to see further progress in goods prices declining as tariff effects fade before cutting again, and added the conditional line, “The rate forecast is conditional on the performance of the economy, so if we don’t see that progress then you won’t see the rate cut.” Markets reacted negatively, with the broad S&P 500 dropping 1.4%. In the Fed’s quarterly projections, officials modestly raised inflation forecasts, now seeing headline inflation at 2.7% by year-end and core inflation also at 2.7%, slightly below the 2.8% January reading; they slightly boosted growth and kept unemployment at 4.4%. Powell also said he had “no intention” of leaving the central bank before a DOJ investigation into his congressional testimony about the Fed’s building renovation is resolved, after a judge on the prior Friday threw out a pair of subpoenas the Justice Department had issued to the Fed and U.S. Attorney Jeannine Pirro said she would appeal. Powell’s term as Fed chair ends May 15, and President Donald Trump has nominated former Fed official Kevin Warsh as replacement, though confirmation has been delayed because key Republican senators oppose the probe; Powell said he had not yet decided whether to remain on the board until his governor term ends in January 2028. One governor, Stephen Miran, dissented for a quarter-point cut.
