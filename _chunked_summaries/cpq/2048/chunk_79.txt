March 7: Israeli airstrikes on Iran and Hezbollah continue into the night | The Times of Israel, 2026-03-06, is a liveblog of Saturday’s war events across Israel, Iran, Lebanon, Iraq, the Gulf and elsewhere, centered on the US-Israeli campaign against Iran, Hezbollah’s rocket and drone fire from Lebanon, and Israel’s widening strikes on Iranian regime infrastructure. Early on, at 3:10 am, it says US President Donald Trump’s administration bypassed congressional review using emergency authority to approve a sale of more than 20,000 bombs to Israel worth around $650 million, with US Secretary of State Marco Rubio determining “an emergency existed” and waiving review; the package included 12,000 BLU-110A/B general purpose 1,000-pound bomb bodies Israel requested, plus BLU-111 500-pound bombs as an amendment, and Israel would also buy an extra $298 million in critical munitions via direct commercial sales, with Repkon USA in Texas named as principal contractor. At 3:06 am, Lebanon’s health ministry said at least four people were killed and ten injured when an Israeli strike hit an apartment in the Ramada hotel building in central Beirut’s Raouche area, housing displaced people from southern Lebanon and Beirut’s southern suburbs. At 3:03 am, the IDF Home Front Command said there were no reports of injuries after an Iranian ballistic missile barrage. At 2:39 am, Kuwait’s defense ministry said fuel tanks at Kuwait International Airport were targeted in a drone attack, and at 2:24 am sirens sounded in central and northern Israel after new missile launches from Iran. At 2:23 am, The Washington Post was cited on two cargo ships owned by Islamic Republic of Iran Shipping Lines (IRISL), a sanctioned state-owned company linked to Tehran’s ballistic missile program, leaving the Chinese port of Gaolan, a chemical storage port experts said handles rocket-fuel ingredients; Carnegie’s Isaac Kardon said China could have delayed them but instead made “a deliberate policy choice.” At 2:05 am a Lebanese security source said at least two were killed in another strike on the Ramada hotel building in central Beirut, the first hit in the heart of the capital since hostilities resumed with Hezbollah. At 1:38 am US Central Command denied Ali Larijani’s claim that several American soldiers had been captured, with spokesperson US Navy Captain Tim Hawkins calling Iran’s regime lies. At 12:42 am Trump called Iran’s regime among “the most evil people on earth,” said “They cut babies’ heads off. They chop women in half,” and appeared to conflate Iran and Hamas while discussing alleged criticism over a desalination plant. At 11:59 pm the IDF said it bombed several fuel depots in Tehran because they served Iran’s military infrastructure, and at 11:54 pm it confirmed strikes on Hezbollah sites in Beirut’s southern suburbs. At 11:53 pm Trump said he did not want Kurdish fighters from Iraq to enter the war, after having said on Thursday he was “all for it,” and repeated that the war “will continue for a while longer,” declined to rule out US ground troops “for a very good reason,” said recovering Iran’s enriched uranium could wait, and dismissed reports of Russian support for Tehran. At 11:48 pm Lebanese media reported fresh Israeli airstrikes in Beirut’s Dahiyeh after an evacuation warning covering four neighborhoods. At 11:44 pm the IDF said it destroyed a Hezbollah rocket launcher used to fire a barrage at the Galilee. At 11:15 pm Reuters said Saudi Arabia warned Iran that continued attacks on its territory and energy sector could push Riyadh to allow US forces to use its bases and to retaliate if energy facilities were hit, while also saying Gulf states had not let the US use their airspace or territory for the Iran strikes. At 11:14 pm the latest Iranian missile at southern Israel was likely intercepted, with no impacts or injuries, the 12th Iranian attack in under 24 hours. At 11:05 pm Iran’s army told Azerbaijan to “expel the Zionists” to prevent insecurity, after Baku said it foiled Iranian plots against a key oil pipeline, a synagogue and Jewish leaders. At 10:56 pm the IDF detected a new Iranian ballistic missile launch; fragments from an intercepted missile hit a home in Lod with damage but no injuries. At 10:52 pm sirens sounded in Nahariya amid Hezbollah rocket fire. At 10:39 pm Fars, citing an Iranian oil ministry source, said Israeli jets hit three oil depots in western Tehran in Kuhak, Shahran and Karaj. At 10:25 pm AFP sources said air defenses intercepted rockets fired at the US embassy in Baghdad; four rockets were launched, three intercepted and one fell in an open area in the embassy’s airbase, with Iraqi Prime Minister Mohammed Shia al-Sudani ordering a hunt for the perpetrators and calling the attack unjustifiable. At 9:51 pm the IDF detected another Iranian ballistic attack expected to trigger central Israel sirens. At 9:47 pm Hezbollah fired a barrage at Kiryat Shmona; multiple interceptions were seen and there were no injuries. At 9:42 pm Netanyahu delivered a televised statement saying the war against Iran would continue “without compromises,” that Israel had “an organized plan with many surprises” for the next phase to destabilize the regime, and that IRGC members were in Israel’s sights; he told Iranians “The moment of truth is drawing near,” said Israel was trying to “free Iran,” warned Lebanon it had to enforce the “2024” ceasefire and disarm Hezbollah, and thanked Trump for the “US-Israel pact” after recalling Trump’s December meeting line, “Bibi, we have to stop Iran at any cost from attaining a nuclear weapon.” At 9:28 pm officials said the Israeli Air Force was striking Iranian oil infrastructure in Tehran, apparently the first time since the current war began that Israel targeted national infrastructure inside Iran. At 9:15 pm more than 150 Iranian nationals, including diplomats and family members, reportedly left Lebanon on a Russian plane after Israeli threats and strikes near the embassy, with another 20 leaving Friday. At 9:03 pm Netanyahu was due to give another TV statement at 9:10 p.m. At 9:03 pm UAE authorities said one person, of Asian nationality, was killed by falling debris from a ballistic-missile interception in Dubai’s Al Barsha area. At 8:58 pm the IDF said a Hamas operative preparing a sniper attack on troops in the Gaza Strip was hit by drone, and at 8:52 pm a missile fired from Iran at central Israel was likely carrying a cluster-bomb warhead, with no injuries, the tenth Iranian launch since midnight. At 8:47 pm the IDF said it launched a second wave of airstrikes in Tehran and revealed that it had recently struck Iran’s two “most central” ballistic missile production sites: the Parchin military complex south of Tehran, where it said it hit explosives factories, raw-material complexes, a missile-engine casting facility and a cruise-missile research/assembly site, and an IRGC ballistic missile production site in Shahrud, some 2,000 kilometers from Israel, which it described as the place where most missiles fired at Israel were manufactured; it said “hundreds” of fighter jets struck the two sites and claimed the strikes set back Iran’s missile production years. At 8:39 pm Turkey’s foreign minister Hakan Fidan warned Iran not to fire more missiles toward Turkey and opposed ethnicity-based civil war scenarios, while explosions were also reported in Baghdad and Erbil. At 8:31 pm the IDF detected another Iranian launch and the Organization of Turkic States in Istanbul condemned attacks on Türkiye and Azerbaijan’s Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic without mentioning Israel, though it supported a two-state solution and aid for Gaza. At 8:29 pm IDF spokesman Brig. Gen. Effie Defrin said the Israeli Air Force had dropped over 7,500 bombs in Iran in the past week, twice the number dropped during Operation Rising Lion, with more than 3,400 strikes carried out. At 8:22 pm medics reported no injuries from the latest Hezbollah rocket barrage at Haifa. At 8:18 pm Channel 12 reported Israeli optimism that the Iranian regime could collapse, with one senior official saying “There is no deadline,” “Russia and China are running away,” and internal rifts between President Masoud Pezeshkian and the IRGC, between military and field forces, were widening. At 8:17 pm police arrested a suspect in Acre after a man in his 50s was found dead at home, amid a day that also saw 57-year-old Suleiman Ziadat shot dead in Nazareth and 28-year-old Muhammad Abed Abu al-Basal die from wounds after a Friday shooting in Tamra. At 8:08 pm the Military Police opened a probe into the killing of a Palestinian by a reservist in Masafer Yatta in the southern West Bank after a violent confrontation; the IDF said two Palestinians were hit by gunfire and one died of wounds. At 8:06 pm B’nai B’rith Canada said two Toronto-area synagogues, BAYT and Shaarei Shomayim, were shot overnight with no injuries, part of a week of attacks on Jewish houses of worship and increased police presence. At 8:02 pm Israeli security officials said Mojtaba Khamenei, son of the late supreme leader Ali Khamenei and a frontrunner to succeed him, was believed alive but wounded after an airstrike, and noted the IDF had hit the Assembly of Experts building in Qom on Tuesday. At 8:01 pm sirens sounded in the Haifa area due to rocket fire from Lebanon. At 7:53 pm the IDF said it demolished two kilometers of Gaza tunnels in the past week and killed five operatives who crossed the Yellow Line, one tunnel in eastern Rafah being used by Hamas operatives against Israeli troops. At 7:50 pm there were no injuries in the ninth Iranian ballistic missile attack since midnight, with the missile hitting an open area and sirens also sounding in Rosh Hanikra due to apparent rocket fire from Lebanon. At 7:34 pm the IDF detected another Iranian launch after a four-hour lull, with sirens expected in northern Israel. At 7:32 pm Hossein Mozafari of Iran’s 88-member Assembly of Experts said the body would meet within “the next twenty-four hours” to choose a new supreme leader, a week after Ali Khamenei’s killing, while urging no rumors. At 7:31 pm Lebanon’s social affairs minister Haneen Sayed said 454,000 people had registered as displaced since the renewed Hezbollah-Israel conflict began, including 112,525 in government shelters. At 7:24 pm the IRGC said it struck the US Juffair base in Bahrain in response to an American attack on the Qeshm desalination plant, using precision-guided solid-fuel and liquid-fuel missiles. At 7:22 pm Saudi Arabia said a ballistic missile targeting Prince Sultan Air Base fell in an uninhabited area. At 7:20 pm Kuwait Petroleum Corporation declared force majeure and began cutting oil output, citing explicit Iranian threats to shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, continued attacks on Kuwait and an “almost total absence” of vessels to ship crude, with February production around 2.6 million barrels per day. At 7:13 pm Bahrain’s interior ministry said an Iranian attack caused a fire in Manama damaging a house and surrounding buildings. At 7:03 pm the IDF Home Front Command said nationwide restrictions remained unchanged until Monday night, with school closures except exceptions, gatherings up to 50 people if a shelter can be reached in time, and workplaces under the same conditions. At 7:02 pm a federal court in New York convicted Pakistani-Iranian Asif Merchant of attempting to assassinate US officials including Trump on behalf of the IRGC; he had entered the US in 2024 to hire hitmen, was arrested months later, and faces up to life. At 7:01 pm Netanyahu said an overnight deep-Lebanon mission that failed to recover Ron Arad’s remains did not change Israel’s “absolute” commitment to missing and captured Israelis. At 6:58 pm The Free Press reported that New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani’s wife, Rama Duwaji, liked an Instagram post calling Hamas rapes a hoax and also liked posts celebrating the Hamas attack, denouncing Israel as a “vile land grab,” praising Columbia building occupiers, and calling Biden “Butcher Biden,” while Mamdani said she was a private person with no formal role. At 6:51 pm the IDF said two Givati Brigade soldiers were moderately wounded in a Hezbollah anti-tank missile attack on a post in southern Lebanon on Friday afternoon; Hezbollah claimed it and the IDF said the cell was struck minutes later, with 13 IDF soldiers wounded in Hezbollah attacks since hostilities resumed on Monday. At 6:38 pm Palestinian media said Ahmed al-Qudra, 34, was killed and his young daughter seriously wounded in an Israeli drone strike on a home in Khan Younis, while Gaza’s Hamas-run health ministry said hospitals received three killed and three wounded by IDF gunfire in 48 hours. At 6:13 pm UAE President Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, visiting injured people, said “The UAE has thick skin and bitter flesh – we are no easy prey” and that the country would protect residents. At 6:12 pm Palestinian reports said Amir Shenaran, 28, was killed and his brother critically wounded by settler gunfire in Wadi al-Rakhim in Masafer Yatta after a dispute over grazing livestock, with WAFA saying this raised the 2026 settler death toll to four and the post-October 7, 2023 total to 40. At 6:05 pm the IDF reiterated an “immediate” evacuation warning to Beirut’s southern suburb, especially those who had not evacuated, saying Hezbollah’s activity compelled it to strike. At 5:41 pm sirens sounded in northern Israel amid rocket and suspected drone attacks from Lebanon. At 5:32 pm the UAE said its air defenses were responding to incoming Iranian missile and drone threats, while explosions were heard in Manama and Doha; later the same update noted six salvos of ballistic missiles had been fired at Israel since midnight with no injuries.
