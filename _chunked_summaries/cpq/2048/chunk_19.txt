### Victory Capital goes public with $8.6 billion rival bid for Janus Henderson | Reuters — 2026-02-26. Victory Capital (VCTR.O), based in San Antonio, went public on Thursday, February 26, 2026, with an $8.6 billion rival bid for Janus Henderson (JHG.N), ratcheting up a bidding war after Janus had already agreed in December 2025 to a $7.4 billion buyout by Nelson Peltz’s Trian and General Catalyst. Victory proposed a cash-and-stock mix of $30 in cash and 0.350 of its shares for each Janus share, valuing Janus at $57.04 per share; Reuters said the combined money manager would have more than $800 billion in assets and an enterprise value of $16 billion. Janus shares rose nearly 6% in afternoon trading while Victory’s stock fell more than 7%. Victory said it had submitted multiple bids in November and December that failed to draw any engagement from Janus’ board, and it argued its offer was “superior” to Trian’s, citing a premium consistent with recent industry deals such as Nuveen’s pending acquisition of Schroders. The Reuters report said the latest proposal valued Janus Henderson at 12.5 times 2026 earnings, compared with the 11 times Trian initially offered according to Evercore analysts, and that the proposal was fully financed through commitments from two investment banks. Victory still needed Janus’ independent board committee to bless the bid as a “superior proposal” before it could terminate the Trian agreement and negotiate, and Trian held about 20.7% of Janus stock according to LSEG. Janus said in December that it had agreed to the takeover after a five-year activist push by Peltz that began in 2020, and Janus itself was formed in 2017 via the merger of Henderson Group and Janus Capital; Reuters also noted the company had struggled with investor outflows and internal disputes since then.
