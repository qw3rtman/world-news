### Security fears rise as Kenya plans to reopen Somalia border | 2026-02-24
President William Ruto of Kenya announced that two border crossings with Somalia would be reopened in April, reviving a long-closed frontier that has been officially shut since 2011 and was previously slated for phased reopening in May 2023 before Kenya reversed course two months later after the killing of five civilians and eight police officers near the frontier in attacks blamed on Al-Shabab, the Somalia-based al-Qaeda-affiliated insurgency. The Kenya-Somalia frontier is 680-kilometer-long (422-mile-long) and also disputed over a potentially oil- and gas-rich chunk of the Indian Ocean. In Beled Hawo, opposite Kenya’s Mandera, trader Mohamed Aden Ali said, “It is a big move,” while community leader Ahmed Barre Diriye said Kenya and Somalia are tied by “trade, education, security, and social ties” and that reopening is crucial because both sides face “severe drought.” Businesswoman Fahmo said she was happy that “after 15 years of closure, the border will finally reopen,” and Ruto posted on X that “It is unacceptable that fellow Kenyans in Mandera remain cut off from their kin and neighbors in Somalia due to the prolonged closure of the Mandera border post.” Somali economic analyst Ahmed Khadar said Kenya could be “the gateway to connecting Somalia to the other members of the East African Community,” noting Somalia joined the EAC in 2024 and joined the EAC passport system this month; Somalia is also rolling out online visas and visas on arrival to boost trade and tourism. The article also described tourists in Mogadishu, including Sherry Roberts and Australian Nick Benady near the Mogadishu Cathedral, badly damaged by Al-Shabab in 2008, and tour boat operator Aweis Ali at Liido Beach citing more visitors sharing activities on TikTok.
