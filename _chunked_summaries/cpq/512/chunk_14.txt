PayPal, founded in the late 1990s as an early mover in digital payments, has struggled to modernize its payment technologies as rivals like Apple and Alphabet seized market share. On Feb 24 PayPal closed up 6.7 per cent at US$47.01 in New York, valuing it at US$43.3 billion (S$54.9 billion), while Stripe, founded by brothers Patrick and John Collison and described as closely held and among the industry’s most valuable companies, announced in an employee tender offer that it had reached a US$159 billion valuation. Stripe president John Collison said PayPal had “definitely had a tough time” amid Apple Pay and Google Pay, though he could not discuss M&A hypotheticals.
