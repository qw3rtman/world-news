Public Storage to Acquire National Storage Affiliates, Creating Significant Value for All Stakeholders – Company Announcement - FT.com, 2026-03-16: Public Storage (NYSE: PSA) and National Storage Affiliates (NYSE: NSA), based in Frisco, Texas & Greenwood Village, Colo., announced an all-stock acquisition valued at an enterprise value of approximately $10.5 billion, with the combined company expected to have pro forma equity market capitalization of approximately $57 billion and total enterprise value of approximately $77 billion. NSA’s portfolio was described as more than 1,000 properties, 69 million rentable square feet, 550,000 units across 37 states and Puerto Rico; PSA said it would wholly-own 488 of the over 1,000 properties on balance sheet, focusing on key Sun Belt and core markets, while a new joint venture with NSA Operating Partnership unitholders would consist of 313 properties with 19.6 million rentable square feet across 28 states and Puerto Rico, an estimated value of approximately $3.3 billion, 80% owned by OP unitholders and 20% by PSA, capitalized with $2.2 billion of property-level secured debt including a mezzanine loan investment from PSA of approximately $240 million and operating at approximately 70% leverage. PSA will repay NSA’s existing bank debt and senior unsecured notes while assuming its existing mortgage debt and Series A, B and A-1 preferred shares and units; Public Storage also arranged committed financing of $4.0 billion from Goldman Sachs Bank USA and Wells Fargo Bank, National Association, split into a $2.0 billion corporate bridge loan and a $2.0 billion JV off-balance sheet bridge loan. The exchange ratio is 0.14, representing an implied offer price of $41.68 per NSA share based on PSA’s closing price on March 13, 2026. Both boards unanimously approved the deal, expected to close in the third quarter of 2026 subject to NSA equity holder approval and other customary conditions. Tom Boyle, incoming CEO, said the PS4.0 strategic vision and PS Next operating model would enable accelerated per-share earnings and cash flow growth, while PSA expects the deal to be accretive to FFO per share within the first year and about $0.35-$0.50 per share accretive after full synergy realization in three to four years; the combined company expects approximately $110 to $130 million of run-rate synergies from revenue enhancements, operating efficiencies, tenant reinsurance uplift and G&A savings, and PSA highlighted its A / A2 credit rating as the highest of any publicly traded U.S. REIT.
