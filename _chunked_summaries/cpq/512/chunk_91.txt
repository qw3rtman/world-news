March 9, 2026, Reuters, “Agilent to acquire Biocare Medical in $950 million all-cash deal”: life sciences firm Agilent Technologies Inc (A.N) said it will acquire privately held clinical pathology firm Biocare Medical Llc in an all-cash deal valued at $950 million. Biocare’s tissue diagnostic products for cancer and infectious diseases will go into Agilent’s Life Sciences and Diagnostics Markets unit, with Agilent expecting revenue growth and profitability within the first year. CEO Padraig McDonnell said Biocare brings more than 300 specialized antibodies and strong new-product capabilities, helping Agilent “better serve a broader base of pathology labs” and expand U.S. and international customer reach. Analyst Vijay Kumar of Evercore ISI said it fits Agilent’s Dako pathology business, while Leerink Partners’ Puneet Souda said Biocare’s staining machines and catalog of more than 300 antibodies would strengthen Agilent’s cancer- and infection-identification tools and help it compete against Danaher Corp’s Leica unit and Roche’s Ventana division. Agilent is buying from Excellere Partners and GHO Capital Partners; close is expected by the fourth quarter of fiscal 2026.
