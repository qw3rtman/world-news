### Mojtaba Khamenei, son of the deceased ayatollah, emerges as frontrunner to become Iran’s new supreme leader | International | EL PAÍS English | 2026-03-06. Ali Falahi’s Madrid piece says Mojtaba Khamenei, 56, son of Iran’s former supreme leader, has emerged as the leading candidate to succeed his father, with his rise linked to hardline Revolutionary Guard commanders, the security apparatus, and continuity of Ali Khamenei’s line. It recalls his birth in Mashhad, his brief 1986 Iran-Iraq War service at age 17 in the Habib ibn Muzahir Battalion of the 27th Mohammad Rasulullah Division, and the post-1989 power shift after Hossein Ali Montazeri was removed from succession, Ruhollah Khomeini died, and Ali Khamenei was chosen; a summer 1989 referendum reduced the supreme leader requirement from marja’ to ijtihad. Mojtaba studied at Alavi school and in the seminary with Seyed Mahmoud Hashemi Shahroudi, Muhammad Taqi Misbah Yazdi, and Lotfollah Safi Golpaygani, and taught dars-e jarey for over a decade before the course was suspended in September 2024, fueling questions over his religious legitimacy. The article says his public image darkened over alleged interference in the 2005 and especially 2009 presidential elections, support for Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, links to the Basij, Revolutionary Guard Intelligence, state broadcasting, and creation of the Ammar Barracks with Mehdi Taeb; the U.S. Treasury sanctioned him in 2019. In February 2026 Bloomberg reported a network of European and Middle East investments and properties via shell companies and banks in the U.K., Switzerland, and UAE, allegedly linked to Iranian oil sales.
