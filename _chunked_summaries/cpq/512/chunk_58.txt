Gold retreats on strong dollar, tempered rate-cut bets | Reuters | 2026-03-03: Gold prices drifted lower on Tuesday as a stronger U.S. dollar and dimming prospects for rate cuts hit bullion amid intensifying inflation fears and worries about a prolonged Middle East conflict. Spot gold was down 3.6% at $5,137.00 an ounce as of 1907 GMT, after hitting an over four-week high in the previous session; U.S. gold futures settled 3.5% lower at $5,123.70. Bob Haberkorn, senior market strategist at RJO Futures, said the move lower appeared driven by “a flight to liquidity - a flight to cash,” with “a strong dollar and bond yields trading higher,” though he said the dip was likely “short-lived” and that “flight to safety flows driven by geopolitical risk should support higher gold and silver prices.” The Iran conflict had entered its fourth day, with explosions rocking Tehran and Beirut and a senior Iranian Revolutionary Guards official saying on Monday the Strait of Hormuz had been closed; crude oil benchmarks jumped over 8% on Tuesday. Fawad Razaqzada of City Index and FOREX.com said damage to energy infrastructure and stalled tanker traffic through Hormuz lifted the risk of sustained strength in oil, gas and refined products, stoking inflation fears and pushing back rate-cut expectations.
