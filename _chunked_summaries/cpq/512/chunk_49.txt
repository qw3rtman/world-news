### How did US launch attack on Iran? Planes, targets and cyberattacks | 2026-03-02: The U.S. war in Iran began Saturday, Feb. 28, with a “massive, overwhelming” strike involving thousands of American servicemembers, hundreds of planes and two aircraft carriers, hitting more than a thousand targets across the country. Gen. Dan Caine, chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, said the military got its “final go order” from President Donald Trump on Feb. 27 at 3:38 p.m., and Trump told troops, “Operation Epic Fury is approved. No aborts. Good luck.” The first actions were cyberattacks meant to jam Iran’s ability to communicate and coordinate. The attack officially began at 9:45 a.m. local time in Tehran after a “trigger event” by Israel’s military. More than 100 U.S. aircraft — fighter jets, tankers, and electronic attack aircraft — lifted off in a “single synchronized wave”; U.S. Navy ships launched Tomahawk missiles at southern Iran; B-2 bombers flew 37 hours round trip from the U.S. mainland to drop “penetrating munitions” on underground facilities. Caine said “more than a thousand targets” were hit in the first 24 hours and called the planning “the culmination of months, and in some cases, years, of deliberate planning.” Iran’s counterattacks killed four Americans, and Kuwait’s military mistakenly shot down three U.S. F-15E fighter jets late Sunday, March 1, with six servicemembers ejected and later recovered in stable condition. Patriot and THAAD systems intercepted “hundreds of ballistic missiles.” Pentagon chief Pete Hegseth confirmed four American servicemembers died after an Iranian missile hit a “tactical operations center,” and the fourth died Monday, March 2, after being severely wounded. Iran’s state media said 555 fatalities had been reported.
