### White House says US Navy has not escorted tanker through Strait of Hormuz - AL-Monitor: The Middle Eastʼs leading independent news source since 2012, 2026-03-10. The White House said Tuesday that the United States had not escorted any oil tanker through the Strait of Hormuz after Energy Secretary Chris Wright’s social media post briefly claimed the “US Navy successfully escorted an oil tanker through the Strait of Hormuz to ensure oil remains flowing to global markets,” then was deleted minutes later; White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt said, “I can confirm that the US Navy has not escorted a tanker or a vessel at this time, though of course that’s an option.” An Energy Department spokesperson said the video clip was removed from Wright’s official X account because it was “incorrectly captioned by Department of Energy staff,” while Iran’s Revolutionary Guards called the claim a “pure falsehood” and said no US Navy vessel had “dared” approach the strait. The false post jolted markets, with oil dropping sharply and then paring losses after deletion; crude had jumped 30 percent on Monday to nearly “$120 per barrel” before retreating. The article says at least 10 oil tankers in or near the Strait of Hormuz were struck, targeted or reported attacked between March 1 and 10, based on UK Maritime Trade Organisation (UKMTO), International Maritime Organization (IMO) and Iranian data, and notes no US ships had been confirmed escorting tankers since the war launched on February 28.
