McGurk, PUBLISHED Mar 10, 2026, 4:32 PM ET, “Analysis: Iran crisis: Three ways this could end | CNN Politics”: McGurk said the Iran crisis was cascading in uncertain ways and centered on President Donald Trump and perhaps Iran’s new supreme leader, Mojtaba Khamenei; Trump on Monday called the war a “short-term excursion” that could end “soon,” but also said it should not end before Iran no longer had “any capacity whatsoever for a very long period of time of developing weaponry that could be used against the US, Israel or any of our allies.” He said the Pentagon-defined military objective was to degrade Iran’s ability to project power outside its borders, striking missiles, missile production facilities, drones, drone manufacturing facilities, naval forces, air forces, command and control structures, and what’s left of the nuclear program; the White House confirmed a four to six weeks timeframe, and officials at a Tuesday Pentagon briefing refused to discuss timeframes. McGurk assigned a base case (60%) of “Contained Iran — think Iraq in the 1990s,” with Iran’s power projection significantly degraded by the end of this month, political structures intact, sanctions remaining until Iran stops pursuing a nuclear program and gives up long-range ballistic missiles, and US and Israel patrolling Iran’s skies. Worst case (30%) was “Emboldened Iran,” in which Trump stops early, leaving reconsolidated power structures, intact military and nuclear capacity, greater Gulf-state threat, higher shipping insurance and capital costs, and potentially deeper US involvement. Best case (10%) was “New Iran — and a new Middle East,” in which strikes on the repressive apparatus, especially the Basij militias and Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, help Iranians retake the streets after a January crackdown that reportedly killed thousands; he noted external military pressure rarely causes rapid regime collapse without internal and armed opposition. The article also said the scenarios were not mutually exclusive, that Mojtaba Khamenei is likely an IRGC figurehead whose claim rests on inheritance from his deceased father, and that the most likely outcome is a weaker, more contained Iran with uncertainty over where things lead. ### 2026-03-10 | US intelligence community ramps up warnings of possible retaliatory attacks by Iran | CNN Politics: The US intelligence community sent private warnings in the past week to American companies and government agencies to harden targets against possible Iranian retaliation, especially cyberattack, after the war with Tehran. A DHS “critical incident note” warned of a heightened threat environment after the killing of Iran’s supreme leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei; it said two top Iranian religious leaders issued separate Farsi-language fatwas calling on Muslims worldwide to take revenge, and cited an IRGC decree saying “the enemy … will no longer have security anywhere in the world, even in their own homes.” The bulletin said the fatwas, Iranian government rhetoric, and online messaging from regime supporters heighten the threat from violent extremists. US officials said no credible homeland threat had been announced, but the FBI went on elevated alert across the country after US and Israel launched strikes. Agencies focused on protecting US energy infrastructure, government targets from sophisticated Iranian cyber threats, and the border. A separate bulletin to private companies warned that claims and calls for cyber attacks by Iranian-aligned groups could increase malicious activity against the financial services sector, historically a priority target and target of opportunity; it also listed public claims of responsibility for attacks on Israel and allied nations. Another notice to US defense contractors warned that Iranian-affiliated cyber actors may target US devices and networks for near-term cyber operations, especially firms with holdings or relationships with Israeli research and defense firms. ### 2026-03-
