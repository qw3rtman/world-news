Reuters said on 2026-02-20 that U.S. President Donald Trump will travel to China from March 31 to April 2, 2026, for a highly anticipated meeting with Chinese President Xi Jinping; the White House official confirmation came as the U.S. Supreme Court struck down many of Trump’s sweeping tariffs, including some China-focused duties. The article says the visit had been expected to focus on extending a trade truce agreed at an October 2025 meeting in South Korea, and that Trump’s last trip to China was in 2017. It specifies that 20-percent tariffs on China’s U.S.-bound exports were imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act, or IEEPA, on national-emergency grounds tied to fentanyl distribution and trade imbalances, while Section 301 and Section 232 duties remain. Trump said he would impose a new 10% global tariff for 150 days, later saying he would raise it to 15%, and he told visiting foreign leaders, “That’s going to be a wild one” and “We have to put on the biggest display you’ve ever had in the history of China.” Taiwan was expected to figure into the talks: Xi raised U.S. arms sales to Taiwan in a February 2026 call, after an October 30, 2025 Busan, South Korea, meeting mostly sidestepped Taiwan.
