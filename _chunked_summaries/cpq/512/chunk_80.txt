The article explained that scheelite, the principal tungsten mineral, is hosted in dense, coarse-grained metamorphic skarn-type rocks; tungsten has a specific gravity of 19.3, “the same hefty weight as gold,” while scheelite is SG 6.1 and mineralised zones may exceed SG 3.5. Managing director and CEO Julian Woodcock cited high tungsten prices of US$1,850/mtu of APT. Gravity data showed coincident anomalies at Linka Main and Conquest, and a big magnetic high on the western side suggested a regional-scale intrusive body, possibly quartz monzonite, with northeast-trending structures crossing it; exposed limestones were magnetically quiet while volcanic rocks were noisier. Viking believed it could follow the mineralised corridor for about 820 metres to where it sub-crops between Linka and Conquest and for a further interpreted 800 metres beneath the southwest volcanic blanket. Phase one covered 2.2 square kilometres of ground magnetics and 0.5 square kilometres of detailed gravity; phase two would expand magnetics by 2.8 square kilometres westward and gravity by 0.35 square kilometres southwest, followed by mapping, rock-chip sampling and a trial gradient array-induced polarisation (GAIP) survey over the northernmost Conquest zone.
