Kairos Pharma Announces Signing of Binding Strategic Asset Acquisition of Exclusive Worldwide Rights to CL-273 From OrbiMed and Torrey Pines Investment-Backed Celyn Therapeutics – Company Announcement - FT.com, Mar 02 2026 11:30 GMT: Kairos Pharma, Ltd. (NYSE American: KAPA), Los Angeles, said it had entered into binding terms to acquire exclusive worldwide rights to CL-273 from Celyn Therapeutics, Inc., a Dover, Delaware privately held biotech backed by OrbiMed and Torrey Pines Investment. John Yu, M.D., called the deal “a pivotal step” for next-generation targeted therapies for EGFR-mutant lung cancer and said it was expected to be value-accretive. CL-273 is described as an investigational, reversible, wild-type-sparing pan-EGFR small-molecule inhibitor discovered with a proprietary AI-driven drug discovery platform, aimed at resistant mutations in EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors and specifically engineered for EGFR-mutant NSCLC. The company cited a 2026 market estimate of $16.2 billion (Future Market Insights), prevalence of EGFR mutations in about 10–15% of NSCLC cases in Western populations and up to 50% in Asian populations (CoherentMI), and said D. Boral Capital, LLC was the sole financial advisor.
