### INTERIM leadership in Iran named after Ali Khamenei's death | 2026-03-01
Iranian state television said on Sunday, via X, that interim leadership had been named after the U.S. and Israel’s joint strike killed Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei; Press TV listed Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian, Guardian Council jurist member Ayatollah Alireza Arafi, and head of the Iranian judiciary Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Eje’i on the interim leadership council. The central event was the U.S. and Israel launching strikes against Iran on Saturday after weeks of tension, with Iran retaliating by attacking Israel and Gulf states including Saudi Arabia and Qatar. Donald Trump announced Saturday afternoon on Truth Social that Khamenei—“one of the most evil people in History”—was dead, saying the death was “Justice for the people of Iran, but for all Great Americans,” and later Iranian state media confirmed it. Under Iranian law, the 88-member Assembly of Experts “must” pick a new supreme leader “as soon as possible,” and the State Department on Saturday urged Americans to “exercise increased caution” and follow the latest security alerts from the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate after “the launch of U.S. combat operations in Iran.” ###
### Who’s running Iran now that the supreme leader is dead? | CNN | 2026-03-01
CNN said the killing of Ayatollah Ali Khamenei by the United States and Israel created a power vacuum in the core of the Iranian regime and triggered a succession process; Khamenei, who had succeeded Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini in 1989, was killed without an officially declared heir. A three-person council formed Sunday to govern until a successor is chosen: Masoud Pezeshkian, Gholamhossein Mohseni Ejei, and Alireza Arafi. The article said Israel claimed a “majority” of Iran’s senior military leaders were killed in Saturday’s strikes, naming armed forces chief of staff Maj. Gen. Abdolrahim Mousavi, IRGC commander Maj. Gen. Mohammad Pakpour, and Defense Council secretary Ali Shamkhani; it also said the temporary council may decide whether to keep delegating defense to Ali Larijani, Iran’s top national security official, and Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, who said the regime had “prepared ourselves for these moments” and “planned for all scenarios.” The 88-member Assembly of Experts, elected every eight years and vetted by the 12-jurist Guardian Council, will choose the new leader; contenders named included Mojtaba Khamenei, Mohammad Mehdi Mirbagheri, Hassan Khomeini, and Alireza Arafi, while Trump urged Iranians on Truth Social to overthrow their government and called on the IRGC to lay down its weapons or “face certain death.” The piece also described the IRGC as comprising between 150,000 and 190,000 troops and the Basij as a volunteer force enforcing Islamic morality. ###
### How Iran's next supreme leader is chosen | AP News | 2026-03-01
AP News, datelined Dubai, United Arab Emirates, explained that the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei after almost 37 years in power raised questions about Iran’s future following an airstrike campaign by the United States and Israel. Under Iran’s constitution, a temporary leadership council formed on Sunday to govern; it includes Iran’s sitting president Masoud Pezeshkian, judiciary chief Gholamhossein Mohseni Ejei, and a Guardian Council member chosen by the Expediency Council.
