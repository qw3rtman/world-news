March 7: Israeli airstrikes on Iran and Hezbollah continue into the night | The Times of Israel — published 2026-03-06. The Times of Israel liveblogged Saturday’s events. At 3:10 am, the US bypassed congressional review to approve a munitions sale to Israel: President Donald Trump’s administration used emergency authority, and US Secretary of State Marco Rubio determined “an emergency existed” to waive review on a package of more than 20,000 bombs worth around $650 million, including 12,000 BLU-110A/B 1,000-pound bomb bodies and BLU-111 500-pound bombs, plus an extra $298 million in direct commercial sales; the contractor was Repkon USA in Texas. At 3:06 am, Lebanon’s health ministry said four were killed and 10 injured when an Israeli strike hit an apartment in the Ramada hotel building in central Beirut’s Raouche area, housing displaced people. At 3:03 am, there were no reports of injuries after an Iranian ballistic-missile barrage. At 2:39 am, Kuwait said fuel tanks at Kuwait International Airport were targeted by drones and its forces were responding to “a wave of hostile drones.” At 2:23 am, The Washington Post reported two cargo ships owned by IRISL, linked to Iran’s missile program, left Gaolan port in China for Iran; Isaac Kardon said China “could have held these vessels at port” but didn’t. At 1:38 am, US Central Command denied Iran’s claim that several American soldiers had been captured. Trump, at 12:42 am on Air Force One, called Iran’s regime “among the most evil people on earth” and said, “They cut babies’ heads off,” while dismissing complaints about a desalination plant. At 11:59 pm and 11:54 pm, the IDF confirmed bombing several oil depots in Tehran and beginning a wave of strikes on Hezbollah sites in Beirut’s southern suburbs; at 11:48 pm, fresh strikes were reported in Dahiyeh. Netanyahu, in a 9:42 pm TV statement, said the war with Iran would continue “unabated,” that Israel had “an organized plan with many surprises,” and that “the moment of truth is drawing near.” He told IRGC members they were in Israel’s sights and told Iranians, “We are trying to free Iran,” while warning Lebanon’s government to disarm Hezbollah. At 9:28 pm, Israeli Air Force strikes on Iranian oil infrastructure in Tehran were described as the first Israeli hit on national infrastructure in Iran since the war began; at 10:39 pm, Iranian oil sources said three depots in Kuhak, Shahran, and Karaj were hit. More than 150 Iranian nationals, including diplomats and families, left Lebanon on a Russian plane after Israeli threats and strikes near the embassy. One person of Asian nationality was killed by falling missile debris in Dubai’s Al Barsha area. The IDF also said a Hamas operative planning a sniper attack in Gaza was struck by drone, and that the latest Iranian missile at 8:52 pm likely carried cluster munitions, with no injuries; at 8:47 pm it launched a second wave on Tehran and revealed strikes on Iran’s two “most central” ballistic-missile production sites: Parchin, including explosives factories and missile-engine facilities, and an IRGC site in Shahrud, some 2,000 kilometers from Israel, where “most of the missiles fired at Israel were manufactured.” Turkey’s Hakan Fidan warned Iran against missiles into Turkish airspace, and the Organization of Turkic States condemned attacks on Türkiye and Nakh
