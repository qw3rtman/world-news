Oil above $100 as as market shrugs off U.S. measures to reduce prices, 2026-03-13: Brent crude oil closed above $100 for the second consecutive session, with Brent futures up 2.67%, or $2.68, to $103.14 per barrel and U.S. crude up 3.11%, or $2.98, to $98.71 per barrel, as the Iran war headed toward its third week and oil tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz was effectively at a standstill amid the U.S.-Israeli conflict with Iran. The article says prices rose despite U.S. and allied steps including the International Energy Agency’s agreed release of 400 million stockpiled barrels, described as “the largest such action in history,” a 30-day waiver for India to buy sanctioned Russian oil, and President Donald Trump considering loosening Jones Act rules to lower costs. Trump said, “We have unparalleled firepower, unlimited ammunition, and plenty of time,” and later urged followers to “watch what happens” to the Iranian regime; Axios reported he told G7 leaders Iran was “about to surrender,” while Iran’s new supreme leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, vowed to keep fighting. Brent futures were up about 10% for the week after a 27.9% rise the prior week, and WTI had its best week since 1983 before ending the week more than 8% higher. Reuters quoted Iran military spokesperson Ebrahim Zolfaqari warning oil could hit $200 a barrel because regional security had been destabilized; Barclays’ Emmanuel Cau said investors still believed in the “Trump put” but warned the longer the Strait stayed closed, the more “stagflationary” markets would become.
