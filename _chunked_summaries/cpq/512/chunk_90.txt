Recession odds jump on Kalshi after oil tops $100, published 2026-03-09, says prediction-market bettors are increasingly expecting the U.S. economy to enter a recession this year as oil prices soar; Kalshi’s market for whether the U.S. goes into a recession in 2026 jumped above 34% on Monday, its highest level since November, after late last week it was under 25%, while a separate Kalshi market put an 11% probability on the next U.S. recession beginning in the first quarter of this year and Polymarket bettors saw a 31% chance by the end of this year. The jump followed U.S. oil prices topping $100 per barrel, a level last passed after Russia’s 2022 invasion of Ukraine, with Middle Eastern producers cutting output, the Strait of Hormuz closed amid the U.S.-Iran war, and West Texas Intermediate crude logging its biggest gain on record last week; analysts warned higher gas and fuel prices could hit consumer and business spending, stocks sold off, and Kalshi participants saw about a 60% chance U.S. gas exceeds $4 this month versus AAA’s $3.48 national average for regular gas.
