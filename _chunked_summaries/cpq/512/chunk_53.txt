Crude may hit $100 if Strait of Hormuz shuts amid Middle East tensions - what it means - The Times of India | 2026-03-02: after US airstrikes on Iran revived fears of a 1970s-style energy shock, the article said Iran produces over 3 million barrels of crude per day, about 3% of world output and the fourth-largest OPEC producer, and warned that if Iran shut the Strait of Hormuz the fallout could be profound. It recalled the 1973–74 Arab OPEC embargo against the United States and other supporters of Israel during the Yom Kippur War, and the 1979 Iranian Revolution, both of which drove prices sharply higher. Energy researcher Saul Kavonic of MST Marquee told CNBC a Hormuz closure could be “three times the severity” of those shocks, send oil into the “triple digits,” and retest LNG highs from 2022; Vandana Hari of Vanda Insights and Bob McNally of Rapidan Energy Group also called it an unprecedented, very serious development. Kpler data said about 13 million barrels per day, roughly 31% of total seaborne oil flows, passed through Hormuz in 2025. The strait, between Oman and Iran, is the main outlet for Gulf crude and refined products; Reuters said Aspides received VHF warnings from Iran’s Revolutionary Guards that “no ship is allowed to pass the Strait of Hormuz,” while vessel movement fell sharply and three ships were reportedly targeted near the Persian Gulf entrance. Alternatives exist but are limited: Saudi Arabia’s 746-mile East-West Pipeline can move up to 5 million barrels per day to a Red Sea terminal, the UAE’s Habshan-Fujairah pipeline handles about 1.5 million barrels per day, Iraq’s Turkey-to-Mediterranean line only serves northern fields, and Kuwait, Qatar and Bahrain have no alternative routes. Iran’s own output was given as roughly 3.3 million barrels per day in 2026, up from under 2 million in 2020, with nearly 90% of exports going to China; Kharg Island is its main shipping and storage hub, capable of loading more than 2 million barrels per day. Analysts cited scenarios from loss of as much as 2 million barrels per day of Iranian exports to a full closure, with Andy Lipow assigning a 33 per cent probability to an attack on Saudi oil infrastructure followed by closure of Hormuz. ###
