France’s far right makes gains in first-round municipal elections ahead of runoff | AP News, 2026-03-17: France’s municipal elections in about 35,000 villages, towns and cities produced gains for Marine Le Pen’s National Rally, especially in southeastern France, with roughly 93% of mayors chosen in the first round and turnout “just over 57%,” higher than in 2020 but lower than 2014; runoff voting was set for March 22. National Rally No. 2 Louis Aliot won by a landslide in Perpignan, Benoît Payan and Franck Allisio were neck and neck in Marseille, Éric Ciotti emerged as favorite in Nice, and the far right also looked strong in Nîmes and Toulon. Negotiations over list mergers were due by 6 p.m. on Tuesday. In Lyon, Green mayor Grégory Doucet allied with France Unbowed against Jean-Michel Aulas, while anti-racism group SOS Racisme urged mainstream unity: “No alliance should be ruled out if it helps prevent this party from taking control of new municipalities,” said president Dominique Sopo. Paris remained unsettled, with Emmanuel Grégoire leading Rachida Dati; Grégoire heads a coalition of Socialists, Greens and Communists and refused a pact with France Unbowed, whose candidate also reached the runoff. Dati, until recently culture minister, faces a September trial on corruption and influence-peddling charges. Outgoing mayor Anne Hidalgo, elected in 2014 and reelected in 2020, declined a third term. The vote was framed as a test before the 2027 presidential race, when Emmanuel Macron cannot seek a third consecutive term; in Le Havre, Édouard Philippe led and sought a runoff win to bolster his presidential standing. Marine Le Pen, convicted last year of embezzlement and barred from office for five years, is appealing; a key decision is expected July 7, and Jordan Bardella would replace her if she cannot run.
