Google wraps up $32B acquisition of cloud cybersecurity startup Wiz | TechCrunch, 2026-03-11: Google officially acquired Israeli cybersecurity firm Wiz for $32 billion in cash, a deal that became Google’s biggest acquisition in its history. Wiz, which provides a security platform that protects major cloud environments by preventing and responding to cybersecurity threats, will join Google Cloud but keep its brand and its commitment to securing customers across all cloud environments. A source said Wiz crossed $1 billion in ARR in 2025. Google said the acquisition is “an investment by Google Cloud to improve cloud security and enable organizations to build fast and securely across any cloud or AI platform.” The multi-cloud environment cited includes Google Cloud, AWS, Azure, and Oracle Cloud, and the companies said they will build a “unified security platform” to help organizations deal with threats faster; Wiz also uses AI to improve threat detection and investigation “at every layer” from code and infrastructure to runtime. Google first approached Wiz in 2024 with a $23 billion offer that CEO Assaf Rappaport rejected, saying he believed the business could become much bigger; talks were revived in early 2025 and Google announced the $32 billion deal in March 2025.
