It says drone and missile attacks damaged or shut multiple key facilities, including refineries and LNG plants in Saudi Arabia, Kuwait and Qatar, while missile debris forced the shutdown of the UAE’s Habshan gas complex and repeated attacks targeted Fujairah export terminal; Bahrain declared force majeure after its Sitra refinery was attacked, and Iraq sharply cut output from its southern oilfields despite avoiding direct strikes. Iran’s military said strikes on its energy infrastructure had led to “a new stage in the war,” and spokesman Ebrahim Zolfaqari warned, “If strikes (on Iran’s energy facilities) happen again, further attacks on your energy infrastructure and that of your allies will not stop until it is completely destroyed.” QatarEnergy’s CEO told Reuters the attacks had knocked out a sixth of Qatar’s LNG export capacity, worth $20 billion a year, and repairs would take three to five years; Israeli media reported an Iranian strike hit oil facilities in Haifa, causing damage but no casualties. The article says disruptions sent Middle East crude benchmarks to record highs, U.S. diesel above $5 a gallon, gasoline to its highest level since late 2023, led Asian refiners to cut runs, and prompted China and South Korea to impose export controls or price caps, while the IEA called for a historic release of 400 million barrels from global reserves. It also explains the difficulty of refueling U.S. fighter jets with aging Boeing KC-135 tankers built in the 1950s and 1960s, spotlighting a mid-air collision over Iraq on Thursday, March 5, that killed all six crew; the military ruled out hostile or friendly fire, but the Islamic Resistance in Iraq claimed responsibility. The KC-46 replacement has had delivery problems and issues with its boom and visual monitoring system, leaving the U.S. reliant on the KC-135 fleet of 153 aircraft; boom operators refuel at 230 miles an hour, 47 feet from the receiving plane. The piece says before the strikes began on February 28 most tankers flew within the U.S. or near Europe, but afterward more flew transatlantic routes to the Middle East. It says Iran continued shipping oil at 1.1 million to 1.5 million barrels per day, then U.S. forces struck Kharg Island, the hub for about 90% of Iran’s oil exports, with Trump saying they had “totally obliterated every military target” there.
