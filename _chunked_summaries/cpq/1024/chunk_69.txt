Ken Paxton, John Cornyn Head to Runoff in Texas GOP Senate Primary - Newsweek, 2026-03-04: Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton and U.S. Senator John Cornyn were projected by the Associated Press to advance to a May runoff in the Texas Republican U.S. Senate primary after neither won a majority on Tuesday; under Texas law a candidate needs more than 50 percent to avoid a runoff, and the three-way field also included U.S. Representative Wesley Hunt. The article framed the race as a test of the Republican Party’s direction in Texas, where the GOP nominee is heavily favored in November and Democrats have not elected a Democrat to statewide federal office in more than three decades. Polling before Election Day showed a narrow Paxton edge but no clear leader: a RealClearPolling average had Paxton at 37.3 percent and Cornyn at 35 percent, Emerson College had Paxton up 4 points, and neither consistently topped 40 percent. Prediction markets were far more bullish on Paxton, with Polymarket pricing him at 82 percent to win versus 17 percent for Cornyn, and Kalshi showing Paxton’s “Yes” shares at 82 cents to Cornyn’s 18 cents. The campaigns and allied super PACs spent tens of millions on TV, digital ads and mail, with Paxton and allies emphasizing his record as attorney general and alignment with President Donald Trump, while Cornyn leaned on his long Senate tenure and support from Republican leadership-aligned groups that spent more than $50 million on advertising and focused heavily on Paxton’s 2023 impeachment.
