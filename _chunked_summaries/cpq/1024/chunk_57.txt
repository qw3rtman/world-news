### Kairos Pharma Announces Signing of Binding Strategic Asset Acquisition of Exclusive Worldwide Rights to CL-273 From OrbiMed and Torrey Pines Investment-Backed Celyn Therapeutics – Company Announcement - FT.com | 2026-03-02. On Mar 02 2026 11:30 GMT, LOS ANGELES-based Kairos Pharma, Ltd. (NYSE American: KAPA), a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on oncology therapeutics, said it had entered into binding terms to acquire CL-273 from Celyn Therapeutics, Inc., a privately held biotechnology company formed in Dover, Delaware and backed by OrbiMed and Torrey Pines Investment; D. Boral Capital, LLC was the sole financial advisor. Chief Executive Officer John Yu, M.D., called the signing of binding terms “a pivotal step” in building Kairos Pharma’s next generation of targeted therapies for EGFR-mutant lung cancer, said the transaction was expected to be “value-accretive,” and described CL-273’s “AI-designed, wild-type-sparing pan-EGFR profile” as potentially best-in-class in a “large, fast-growing $16.2 billion lung cancer market.” CL-273 is described as an investigational, reversible, wild-type-sparing pan-EGFR small-molecule inhibitor discovered using a proprietary AI-driven drug discovery platform; it targets resistant mutations that develop with EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors and is specifically engineered for EGFR-mutant non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). The announcement cites Future Market Insights for the $16.2 billion 2026 market estimate and CoherentMI for prevalence data showing EGFR mutations in approximately 10–15% of NSCLC cases in Western populations and up to 50% in Asian populations. Yu said Kairos was partnering with an OrbiMed-backed innovator and believed the company was positioned to deliver a highly differentiated EGFR inhibitor to patients worldwide. The release also detailed Kairos’s existing lead candidate ENV-105, an antibody targeting CD105 to reverse drug resistance and disease relapse and restore the effectiveness of standard therapies; as of the release, ENV-105 was in a Phase 2 clinical trial for castrate-resistant prostate cancer and a Phase 1 trial for non-small cell lung cancer and had not been approved as safe or effective by the FDA or any comparable regulator. Celyn Therapeutics said it brings deep domain expertise in small-molecule oncology drug development and was created with backing from OrbiMed and Torrey Pines Investment. Vitesse Energy, Inc. (NYSE: VTS), headquartered in GREENWOOD VILLAGE, Colo., said on Mar. 2, 2026 that it had signed an accretive all-stock acquisition, reported fourth quarter and full year 2025 financial and operating results, and issued 2026 guidance. The company signed a definitive agreement on Mar. 1, 2026 to acquire non-operated assets in the Powder River Basin of Wyoming—specifically in Campbell and Converse Counties—for $35 million of Vitesse common stock, with an effective date of January 1, 2026; the assets include over 6,000 net acres, approximately 1,400 Boe per day of expected net production for 2026, and 29 net undeveloped locations operated primarily by EOG Resources and Continental Resources, and the deal was expected to close at the beginning of the second quarter and be accretive to earnings, operating cash flow, free cash flow, and net asset value per share. For full-year 2025, Vitesse reported net income of $25.3 million, Adjusted Net Income of $30.4 million, Adjusted EBITDA of $179.3 million, cash flow from operations of $170.3 million, Free Cash Flow of $48.9 million, average production of 17,444 barrels of oil equivalent (“Boe”) per day with 65% oil, total cash development capital expenditures and acquisition costs of $127.7 million, total debt of $124.5 million, and a Net Debt to Adjusted EBITDA ratio of 0.69; total revenue was $273,989,000, including $244,414,000 of oil revenue and $29,575,000 of natural gas revenue, with oil averaging $59.14 per Bbl before hedging and $62.95 per Bbl with hedging, natural gas averaging $2.21 per Mcf before hedging and $2.31 per Mcf with hedging. Fourth-quarter 2025 results included a net loss of $0.7 million, Adjusted Net Income of $0.2 million, Adjusted EBITDA of $36.7 million, production of 17,653 Boe per day, and total revenue of $65.5 million; the company said oil represented 63% of production and 93% of revenue in the quarter, with realized prices before hedging of $53.54 per Bbl for oil and $1.14 per Mcf for gas and with hedging of $60.03 per Bbl and $1.23 per Mcf. On reserves, Vitesse said total proved reserves at December 31, 2025 increased 19% from December 31, 2024 to 47.8 million Boe (71% proved developed), driven by the acquisition of Lucero Energy Corp. in March 2025; proved developed reserves rose to 34.0 million Boe, proved undeveloped reserves to 13.8 million Boe, Standardized Measure was $439.0 million and PV-10 was $472.7 million, with the decline from year-end 2024 blamed primarily on lower net realized oil prices. Cash at December 31, 2025 was $1.3 million, revolving credit facility borrowings were $124.5 million, and total liquidity was $126.8 million. For 2026, Vitesse guided to two-stream production of 16,000 - 17,500 Boe per day and total cash capital spending of $50 - $80 million, including the Powder River Basin acquisition beginning in the second quarter. It also declared a first quarter 2026 cash dividend of $0.4375 per share on February 25, 2026, payable March 31, 2026, after paying a fourth quarter 2025 dividend of $0.5625 per share on December 31, 2025.
