Brent futures rose 2.67%, or $2.68, to close at $103.14 per barrel, while U.S. crude oil gained 3.11%, or $2.98, to settle at $98.71 per barrel. The article said prices were rising despite the International Energy Agency agreeing to release 400 million stockpiled barrels, described as the largest such action in history, and despite a U.S. 30-day waiver for India to buy sanctioned Russian oil plus Donald Trump considering loosening Jones Act rules to lower costs. Oil prices notched another week of gains, with Brent up about 10% after last week’s 27.9% rise, the biggest weekly gain since Covid-19 in 2020, and WTI finishing the week more than 8% higher after its best week since 1983.
