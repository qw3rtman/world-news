### After major restructuring, Ubisoft CEO Yves Guillemot says multiple Far Cry and Assassin's Creed games are in development: 'We have a solid...' - The Times of India — 2026-02-23. Ubisoft, after a restructuring that reorganised it into five “creative houses,” said new Far Cry and Assassin’s Creed games were indeed in development. Speaking to Variety, CEO and co-founder Yves Guillemot said the Tencent-backed subsidiary Vantage Studios was part of the updated structure intended to support ongoing development across major brands. Guillemot said, “We have a solid pipeline underway across Vantage Studios. Under the Assassin's Creed brand, several titles are in development, spanning both single-player and multiplayer experiences, with the ambition to further grow a community that exceeded 30 million players last year.” He also confirmed “two very promising projects” for Far Cry. The article says rumours pointed to Project Blackbird, expected to be the next mainline Far Cry entry with a “non-linear” story about the kidnapping of the protagonist’s family, unfolding over “72 in-game hours,” roughly “24 hours in real time,” and to Project Maverick, a standalone multiplayer extraction-based shooter set in “the Alaskan wilderness” with “mechanics such as permadeath, a backpack system, contracts, and more.” For Assassin’s Creed, known upcoming projects include Project Hexe and a Black Flag remake, while Insider Gaming reported Ubisoft had cancelled two planned Assassin’s Creed titles, including “Assassin's Creed Singularity,” described as a PC and mobile virtual trading card game with Web3 and blockchain elements. Ubisoft is also developing a Far Cry anthology series with It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia’s Rob McElhenney and Alien Earth creator Noah Hawley; McElhenney, also known as Mac from Mythic Quest and Ian Grimm, is expected to star as well as produce.
