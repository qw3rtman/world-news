Industry sources said IMM Private Equity emerged as the preferred bidder, securing exclusive negotiating rights to acquire a 51 percent stake held by the company’s largest shareholder, A-Hana, which is controlled by Bluenet, the Daewoong Group’s founding-family company that holds a 55.84 percent stake. Industry officials expect IMM to focus on global commercialization of CGBio’s bone graft substitutes. At Linka, the principal tungsten mineral scheelite is hosted in dense, coarse-grained metamorphic skarn-type rocks; skarn forms by chemical alteration of limestone or dolomite by high-temperature hydrothermal fluids from magma intrusions. Tungsten has a specific gravity (SG) of 19.3, “the same hefty weight as gold,” while scheelite has an SG of 6.1; mineralised zones may often exceed an SG of 3.5, producing sharp density contrasts in gravity surveys. Managing director and chief executive officer Julian Woodcock cited “high tungsten prices of US$1,850/mtu of APT” as a reason the company is advancing rapidly. Phase one gravity data showed coincident anomalies across known mineralisation at the adjacent Linka Main and Conquest prospects, and magnetic surveying mapped key boundaries, with exposed limestones magnetically quiet and volcanic rocks noisier. A big magnetic high on the western side of the survey grid may be a regional-scale intrusive body, possibly a quartz monzonite, and Viking is targeting contact-style mineralisation where the intrusive body abuts lower-magnetic rocks like limestones. The company also identified northeast-trending structures across the interpreted intrusion, suggesting pathways beneath younger volcanic cover. By combining gravity and magnetic datasets, Viking thinks it can follow the mineralised corridor for about 820 metres to where it sub-crops between Linka and Conquest and for a further interpreted 800 metres beneath the southwest volcanic blanket. Phase one included 2.2 square kilometres of ground magnetics and 0.5 square kilometres of detailed gravity surveying; phase two will extend magnetics by 2.8 square kilometres to the west and the gravity grid by 0.35 square kilometres to the southwest. After phase two, Viking plans to get boots back on the ground to map and conduct rock-chip sampling, and it has flagged a trial gradient array-induced polarisation (GAIP) survey over the northernmost Conquest mineralised zone.
