He is now running as the Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP) prime ministerial candidate in Nepal’s first election since the September 2025 Gen Z protests that forced KP Sharma Oli to resign and led to Sushila Karki’s brief interim term; Balen backed Karki instead of taking the interim job, a tactical move the article says is now clear. He is contesting Jhapa-5, about 300km (186 miles) southeast of Kathmandu, directly against Oli, and the piece notes Jhapa is an Oli/CPN-UML stronghold. The RSP is described as a centrist party formed less than four years ago that won 10 percent of the national vote in 2022. Balen’s rhetoric and online presence are central: in a November 2025 midnight Facebook post he wrote, “F*** America, F*** India, F*** China, F*** UML, F*** Congress, F*** RSP, F*** RPP, F*** Maobaadi. He avoids mainstream media, communicating via podcasts, TV judge roles, and social media, where he has 3.5 million Facebook followers, 1 million on Instagram, 400,000 on X and nearly 1 million on YouTube.
