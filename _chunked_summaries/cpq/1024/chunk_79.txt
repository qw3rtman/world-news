March 7: Israeli airstrikes on Iran and Hezbollah continue into the night | The Times of Israel, 2026-03-06, is a liveblog of Saturday’s events as the US-Israel war against Iran broadened into Lebanon and across the Gulf: at 3:10 am the US bypassed congressional review to approve a munitions sale to Israel worth around $650 million, with US President Donald Trump’s administration using emergency authority and US Secretary of State Marco Rubio determining that an emergency existed; the package included more than 20,000 bombs, including 12,000 BLU-110A/B general purpose 1,000-pound bomb bodies requested by Israel, plus BLU-111 500-pound bombs as an amendment to a previous sale, and an extra $298 million in critical munitions via direct commercial sales, with Repkon USA of Texas named as principal contractor. At 3:06 am Lebanon’s health ministry said at least four people were killed and 10 injured when an Israeli strike hit an apartment in the Ramada hotel building in Beirut’s Raouche area, housing displaced people; at 3:03 am the IDF said there were no reports of injuries in an Iranian ballistic missile barrage and civilians could leave bomb shelters. At 2:39 am Kuwait’s defense ministry said fuel tanks at Kuwait International Airport were targeted in a drone attack on vital infrastructure, with forces responding to “a wave of hostile drones”; at 2:24 am sirens sounded in central and northern Israel after new Iranian missile launches, and at 2:23 am The Washington Post reported two cargo ships owned by IRISL, the US-, UK- and EU-sanctioned Islamic Republic of Iran Shipping Lines linked to Tehran’s ballistic missile program, left the Chinese port of Gaolan carrying chemicals used as rocket-fuel ingredients, with Isaac Kardon saying China could have used bureaucratic tools to stop them but made a deliberate policy choice during an active war. At 2:05 am another strike hit the Ramada hotel apartment in central Beirut, with at least two killed, the first attack in the heart of the capital since hostilities resumed; at 2:02 am rocket sirens sounded in northern Israel, especially Nahariya, with no injuries. At 1:38 am US Central Command spokesman US Navy Captain Tim Hawkins denied Iranian security chief Ali Larijani’s claim that several American soldiers had been taken prisoner, calling it a lie. At 12:42 am Trump aboard Air Force One called Iran’s regime “among the most evil people on earth,” accused it of supporting Hamas and saying they “cut babies’ heads off,” while dismissing Iranian complaints about a desalination plant and conflating Iran with Hamas; the liveblog noted the initial beheading testimony on October 7 was never verified. At 11:59 pm the IDF confirmed bombing several Iranian fuel depots in Tehran, saying the tanks served military entities and the strike deepened damage to the regime’s military infrastructure; at 11:54 pm it confirmed a wave of airstrikes on Hezbollah sites in Beirut’s southern suburbs, and at 11:53 pm Trump said he did not want Kurdish fighters from Iraq to go into Iran because “the war is complicated enough,” though he said they were willing and that the objective would be “to win,” while also discussing a girls’ elementary school strike in southern Iran that reportedly killed 150 people and saying Russia was not backing Tehran. At 11:48 pm Lebanese media reported fresh Israeli airstrikes in Beirut’s southern suburbs after evacuation warnings for four major Dahiyeh neighborhoods; at 11:44 pm the IDF said it had destroyed a Hezbollah rocket launcher used in barrages at the Galilee. At 11:15 pm Reuters reported Saudi Arabia told Iran continued attacks on the kingdom and its energy sector could push Riyadh to respond in kind, with Foreign Minister Prince Faisal bin Farhan telling Abbas Araghchi that neither Riyadh nor other Gulf states had let the US use their airspace or territory for strikes on Iran. At 11:14 pm the latest Iranian ballistic missile toward southern Israel was likely intercepted, the 12th attack in under 24 hours, with no impacts or injuries. At 11:05 pm Iran’s army told Azerbaijan to “expel the Zionists” to prevent insecurity, a day after Baku said it foiled Iranian plots against a key oil pipeline, a synagogue and Jewish leaders, and after four drones crashed into Nakhchivan on Thursday. At 10:56 pm the IDF detected another Iranian ballistic missile launch, with sirens expected in southern Israel, and said fragments from an intercepted missile hit a home in Lod with no injuries; at 10:52 pm sirens sounded in Nahariya amid a Hezbollah rocket attack. At 10:39 pm a source in Iran’s oil ministry told Fars that Israeli jets hit three oil depots in western Tehran in Kuhak, Shahran and nearby Karaj. At 10:25 pm AFP sources said air defenses intercepted rockets fired at the US embassy in Baghdad, the first such attack since the war began, with Iraqi PM Mohammed Shia al-Sudani calling it a “terrorist act.” At 9:51 pm the IDF detected a fresh Iranian ballistic attack with sirens expected in central Israel; at 9:47 pm sirens sounded in Kiryat Shmona after a Hezbollah barrage, with multiple interceptions and no injuries. At 9:42 pm Netanyahu said Israel had “an organized plan with many surprises” to destabilize and change the Iranian regime, urged IRGC members to surrender, said “The moment of truth is drawing near,” and told Iranians “we are trying to free Iran,” while warning Lebanon to enforce the 2024 ceasefire and disarm Hezbollah or face disaster. At 9:28 pm officials said the Israeli Air Force was striking Iranian oil infrastructure in Tehran, apparently the first attack on Iranian national infrastructure since the war began. At 9:15 pm Reuters said more than 150 Iranian nationals, including diplomats and families, had left Lebanon on a Russian plane after Israeli threats and strikes near Iran’s embassy; another 20 left Friday, and Lebanese authorities had also been planning expulsions. At 9:03 pm Netanyahu was due to give a TV statement at 9:10 pm amid the widening campaign. At 9:03 pm the UAE said one person of Asian nationality was killed by falling debris from a ballistic missile interception in Dubai’s Al Barsha area. At 8:58 pm the IDF said a Hamas operative planning a sniper attack on southern Gaza troops was killed in a drone strike; at 8:52 pm the latest Iranian missile over central Israel was likely carrying a cluster-bomb warhead, the 10th since midnight, with no injuries and civilians cleared from shelters, and the IDF said such warheads open in descent and spread dozens of munitions within about 8km.
