Spot gold was down 3.6% at $5,137.00 an ounce as of 1907 GMT, after hitting an over four-week high in the previous session; U.S. gold futures settled 3.5% lower at $5,123.70. Bob Haberkorn, senior market strategist at RJO Futures, said the move lower appeared driven by “a flight to liquidity - a flight to cash,” adding, “We have a strong dollar and bond yields trading higher,” and that the dip would likely be short-lived because flight-to-safety flows from geopolitical risk should support higher gold and silver prices. The geopolitical backdrop centered on the Iran conflict entering its fourth day, with explosions rocking Tehran and Beirut and a senior Iranian Revolutionary Guards official saying on Monday the Strait of Hormuz had been closed; crude oil benchmarks jumped over 8% on Tuesday. Fawad Razaqzada, market analyst at City Index and FOREX.com, said damage to energy infrastructure and stalled tanker traffic through Hormuz had lifted the risk of sustained strength in oil, gas and refined products, stoking inflation fears and pushing back rate-cut expectations, even though gold is typically preferred in low-rate environments because it yields no interest. Spot gold had gained 19% this year after a 64% surge in 2025. Silver fell 6.6% to $83.50 an ounce after climbing to a more than four-week high on Monday; platinum lost 8.4% to $2,108.51 and palladium shed 5.6% to $1,667.41.
