Powell said the Fed would need to see further progress in goods prices declining as tariff effects fade before cutting again, and investors responded by sending the S&P 500 down 1.4%. Powell said inflation was 2.8% in January, up from 2.3% nearly a year earlier, while core inflation was 3.1%, and cited a still-stubborn price backdrop plus weak labor data, including 92,000 jobs lost in February after 130,000 gained in January.
