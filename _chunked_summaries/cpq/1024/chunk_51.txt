### US, Israel exchange strikes with Iran and its allies | AP News | 2026-03-02: Israel and the United States pounded Iran on Monday in a campaign U.S. President Donald Trump said would likely take “four to five weeks” but that he was prepared “to go far longer than that,” with Trump also saying at a White House event that the operation had been projected from the beginning to last four to five weeks and that he would not get “bored” continuing it. The war, which began when U.S. and Israel attacked Iran on Saturday, spread across the Middle East: Iranian-backed Hezbollah in Lebanon fired missiles into Israel for the first time in more than a year, and Israel responded with strikes on Beirut’s southern suburbs and southern Lebanon; the Lebanese Health Ministry said those Israeli airstrikes killed at least 52 people and wounded 154 in one wave, while another later report said overnight strikes in Lebanon killed at least 31 and displaced thousands. Israeli strikes hit a building housing Al-Manar channel studios in Beirut’s southern suburbs after an evacuation warning, with the Israeli military saying it targeted “Hezbollah command centers and weapons storage facilities in Beirut,” and an Israeli airstrike near the old Iranian embassy compound in Beir Hassan killed the top commander in Lebanon of the Quds Brigades, Adham Adnan al-Othman of Palestinian Islamic Jihad. The article says Hezbollah’s retaliation was “a reaction to the aggression” and that confrontation is “a legitimate right.” The U.S. death toll rose to 6 troops after two previously unaccounted-for service members were confirmed dead, and U.S. Central Command said 18 American service members were seriously wounded; a separate line said six Army soldiers were killed in Kuwait, all from the same logistics unit, after an Iranian weapon got past allied air defenses and hit a fortified tactical operations center. Trump and Secretary of State Marco Rubio said the objectives were not regime change but to destroy Iran’s ballistic missile capability, prevent it from rebuilding that capacity, stop its nuclear program, and end its ability to arm proxy militias; Rubio said “the hardest hits are yet to come” and “the next phase will be even more punishing,” while Trump said the U.S. had already “knocked out” 10 ships and was targeting Iran’s navy. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, in his first public briefing since the attacks, defended the operation as designed to reduce Iran’s navy and end its nuclear and missile ambitions; he said Iranian officials had stalled during negotiations and that the strikes had “nothing to do with nation-building,” though he also said ground troops were not ruled out and the war could last “four weeks, two weeks, six weeks.” House Speaker Mike Johnson called it a “defensive operation,” while Sen. Mark Warner said “there was no imminent threat to the United States of America by the Iranians,” only to Israel, and Chuck Schumer called the briefing “completely and totally insufficient.” The State Department urged U.S. citizens in more than a dozen Middle Eastern countries, including Iran, Iraq, Jordan, Lebanon and Israel, to “DEPART NOW,” and embassy staff left the U.S. Embassy compound in Amman, Jordan, “due to a threat,” while Jordanian police told residents near the embassy to stay indoors and close windows. Bahrain’s U.N. ambassador Jamal Alrowaiei told the Security Council that Iranian attacks on civilian facilities and residential areas had forced schools to close, as U.N. officials said 30 million children in the Middle East and north Africa are out of school or not receiving formal education. COGAT said Israel would reopen the Kerem Shalom Crossing into Gaza “for the gradual entry of humanitarian aid,” after having closed Gaza crossings because it could not safely operate under fire; the U.N. said closure was stretching food, water and fuel stocks and reducing water production in Gaza City to as little as two liters a day per person. Markets reacted sharply, with oil prices leaping and the S&P 500 falling as much as 1.2% before closing up less than 0.1%, while the Dow rose 0.1% and the Nasdaq 0.4%. Other regional fallout included a drone strike on Abu Dhabi’s Musaffah fuel terminal that was quickly contained without injuries, Cyprus reporting a drone hit on RAF Akrotiri with “minimal” damage and four Greek F-16s landing there, and NATO chief Mark Rutte saying the war was crucial for European security and that he unreservedly approved Trump’s decision to attack Iran. Iran said it shot down 20 “enemy drones,” while the UAE said it intercepted 9 ballistic missiles, 6 cruise missiles and 148 drones; Iranian state media and officials also circulated claims of deadly airstrikes, including Abbas Araghchi’s post showing graves for more than 160 girls killed in a school strike in southern Iran, which Iran said killed at least 165 people and dozens more were wounded, though Israel said it was not aware of strikes there and the U.S. said it was looking into the reports. On the Israeli side, three young siblings Yaakov, 16, Avigail, 15, and Sarah Biton, 13, were buried at the Mount of Olives after an Iranian missile hit a synagogue shelter in Beit Shemesh, killing nine people there and bringing Israel’s death toll to 11; 65 people were hospitalized, including two seriously wounded. Trump said he had “no yips” about boots on the ground if necessary, but Vice President JD Vance had earlier said there was “no chance” of a drawn-out war. Governments and entities around the world issued reactions: Spain’s Pedro Sánchez called the attack “unjustifiable,” Belarus’s Alexander Lukashenko called Khamenei’s killing a “treacherous attack,” Russia’s Vladimir Putin spoke to Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman about the “American-Israeli armed aggression,” and veterans, voters, and two Trump supporters in Texas split sharply on whether Trump was acting with a plan.
