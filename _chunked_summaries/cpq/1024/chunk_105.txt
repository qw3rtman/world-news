### Public Storage to Acquire National Storage Affiliates, Creating Significant Value for All Stakeholders – Company Announcement - FT.com | 2026-03-16 | Public Storage (NYSE: PSA) and National Storage Affiliates (NYSE: NSA) announced an all-stock agreement under which PSA will acquire NSA in a deal valued at approximately $10.5 billion enterprise value, with the combined company expected to have a pro forma equity market capitalization of approximately $57 billion and total enterprise value of approximately $77 billion. NSA’s portfolio is described as more than 1,000 properties, 69 million rentable square feet, and 550,000 units across 37 states and Puerto Rico, while Public Storage said it owned and/or operated 3,533 self storage facilities in 40 states and, at December 31, 2025, also held a 35% common equity interest in Shurgard Self Storage Limited. Under the terms, NSA common shares and operating partnership (“OP”) units will receive 0.14 of a share of PSA common stock or partnership units per NSA share or unit, implying $41.68 per NSA share based on PSA’s closing share price on March 13, 2026. Both boards of trustees unanimously approved the transaction, which is expected to close in the third quarter of 2026, subject to NSA equity holder approval and other customary closing conditions. Before closing, PSA and limited partners in NSA’s OP will form a new joint venture consisting of 313 properties on NSA’s operating platform, covering 19.6 million rentable square feet across 28 states and Puerto Rico and valued at about $3.3 billion; OP unitholders are expected to own approximately 80% of the JV at inception and PSA 20%, with the JV capitalized with $2.2 billion of property-level secured debt including a mezzanine loan investment from PSA of approximately $240 million and expected to operate at approximately 70% leverage. PSA will exclusively manage the JV and earn customary property management, asset management and tenant reinsurance income. PSA will repay NSA’s existing bank debt and senior unsecured notes while assuming its existing mortgage debt and Series A, B and A-1 preferred shares and units, and it has arranged committed financing of $4.0 billion from Goldman Sachs Bank USA and Wells Fargo Bank, National Association, split into a $2.0 billion corporate bridge loan and a $2.0 billion JV off-balance sheet bridge loan that will become permanent secured mortgage financing. Tom Boyle, incoming CEO of Public Storage, said the transaction would expand the platform “strategically and accretively” under the PS4.0 strategic vision and PS Next operating model, while NSA CEO David Cramer said the deal would deliver a meaningful premium and allow shareholders and OP unitholders to participate in upside; Cramer also cited NSA’s transformation and the Participating Regional Operator model pioneered by Arlen Nordhagen. Public Storage framed the acquisition as its first PS4.0 value creation milestone, citing its A/A2 credit rating as the highest of any publicly traded U.S. REIT, expected leverage-neutrality after synergies, accretion to FFO per share within the first year, approximately $0.35-$0.50 per share accretion upon full synergy realization in three to four years, and anticipated run-rate synergies of about $110 to $130 million from revenue enhancements, operating efficiencies, tenant reinsurance uplift and G&A savings; it said the combined portfolio would include more than 1,000 properties and 550,000 units under the Public Storage brand, with greater exposure to the Sun Belt and other high-growth geographies. Advisors named included Goldman Sachs & Co. LLC, Wells Fargo and Eastdil Secured for PSA; Morgan Stanley & Co.
