### Russia is providing intelligence to Iran on the location of U.S. forces, sources say | March 6, 2026, 12:31 PM EST / Updated March 6, 2026, 5:55 PM EST. In this Washington report by Dan De Luce, Monica Alba, Gordon Lubold and Julie Tsirkin, four sources with knowledge of the matter said Russia is providing intelligence to Iran on the location of U.S. forces in the Middle East, including data from satellites and other intelligence, as Tehran launches missile and drone attacks on American bases and other targets after the United States and Israel launched an air war against the Iranian regime last Saturday. The assistance could help Iran locate American warships, radar, and other communication systems, but sources said there is “no indication” Moscow is helping direct specific Iranian missile or drone strikes. The report said The Washington Post first reported on Russia’s assistance. It noted public images of Iranian strikes showing U.S. military radar, satellite dishes and communication infrastructure under attack at American bases in the Persian Gulf, and quoted Nicole Grajewski, an expert on Russia and Iran cooperation and assistant professor at Sciences Po in Paris, saying Russia can provide information faster and with more precision because Iran has only a small number of military satellites while Russia has a large network. Grajewski said the Iranian attacks appear more precise than in the 12-day war between Iran and Israel in June and more focused on radar sites and communication posts, appearing to target “command and control,” and resembling Russia’s air campaign in Ukraine with swarms of drones followed by ballistic missile attacks. In an NBC News interview with Tom Llamas on Thursday, Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi said Russia and China were supporting Iran “politically and otherwise,” that long-standing military cooperation with Russia “is not a secret,” but he would not give details during the war.
