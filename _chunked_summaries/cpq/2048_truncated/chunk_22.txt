Ukraine claws back southern territory as Russia’s war enters fifth year | Russia-Ukraine war News | Al Jazeera — 2026-02-27: On the fourth anniversary of Russia’s invasion, Ukraine reported gains in southern Ukraine and stronger air defences amid two drone-and-missile barrages in the past week. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said Ukrainian forces had regained “300 square kilometres (115 square miles)” of territory in the south on Saturday, while commander in chief Oleksandr Syrskii said two days later the southern gains were “400sq km (155 square miles)” and had come since “the end of January.” Zelenskyy said, “You can’t say that we’re losing the war … The question is whether we will win.” The Institute for the Study of War, a Washington-based think tank, said Ukraine’s successes came partly because Ukraine persuaded Starlink to disconnect illegal Russian terminals in the theatre and because the Kremlin partly disabled Telegram; Russian troops had used both for essential military communications. The ISW said the limited Ukrainian counterattacks may have thrown Russian preparations for a spring offensive off balance, and noted open-source intelligence suggesting Russia’s campaign had been flagging for a long time. European leaders said that over the past three years Russia had seized “1.5 percent of Ukraine or less” at a cost of “hundreds of thousands of lives.” The battle for Pokrovsk, a city in Ukraine’s eastern Donetsk region with a pre-war population of “60,000,” appeared to have ended in Russian victory after a two-year fight; the ISW said Pokrovsk had been operationally significant as a logistics hub, but Russian strikes had denied Ukrainian forces the ability to fully leverage it “as early as July 2025,” and it had “long ceased” to be operationally significant by “Winter 2025.” On Tuesday, the war’s fourth anniversary, Zelenskyy said Vladimir Putin “understands he is not capable of defeating Ukraine on the battlefield,” and that the “second army in the world” was fighting “against apartment buildings and power plants,” referring to Russia’s aerial campaign against the “four-fifths of Ukraine that remain free.” He said “the Air Force has started performing better.” During the week Russia attacked with “just more than 1,500 drones and at least 90 missiles.” On Sunday Russia launched “197 drones and 50 missiles”; Ukraine downed all but “26 drones and 31 missiles.” On Thursday Russia launched “420 drones and 39 missiles”; Ukraine neutralised “90 percent of the drones and 30 missiles.” Defence ministry adviser Serhiy Beskrestnov said, “All the ballistics that come to us are made at the end of 2025 – the beginning of 2026. That is, we are actually being attacked with missiles from the factory.” Ukraine’s Air Force said it had destroyed “3,855 long-range missiles” and “67,000 long-range drones” used since the start of the war. Ukraine’s strategic campaign against Russian weapons and fuel supply chains scored hits during the week: on February 21, Ukrainian-made FP-5 “Flamingo” missiles struck the Votkinsk missile factory in Russia’s Udmurtia, “about 1,200km (745 miles)” from the border, which missile expert Fabian Hoffman called “the first time that Ukraine has successfully struck a core target of Russia’s missile industry directly using a heavy missile capability.” Ukraine’s General Staff said the site makes a series of ballistic missiles including the “unstoppable” Oreshnik intermediate ballistic missile, now stationed in Belarus and repeatedly threatening Europe; satellite imagery showed at least one machine-building plant was hit, though the extent of interior damage was uncertain. On the same day drones hit the Neftogorsk Gas Processing Plant in Samara, damaging two gas condensate stabilisation plants. On Monday Ukraine struck the Kaleykino Oil Pumping Station in Tatarstan, described as a key facility for filling the Druzhba oil pipeline to Hungary and Slovakia. On Wednesday Ukraine struck a chemicals plant in Smolensk, causing explosions and fires. Ukraine marked the anniversary with survival, with Zelenskyy saying, “Ukraine has come a long way – from the point when we were being given body armour to the point when we ourselves produce more than three million [first-person view] drones a year,” while European Commission president Ursula Von Der Leyen and European Council president Antonio Costa stood beside him. Zelenskyy said, “Europe has stood with us throughout these difficult, really difficult years, and our people trust Europe.” The EU has sent Ukraine “195 billion euros ($230bn) to date” and voted to send another “90 billion euros ($106bn)” over the next two years. ###

Neogen’s genomics business serves customers in “more than 120 countries” through five laboratories in the United States, Brazil, Australia, China and the United Kingdom, plus an office in Canada; it uses a genotyping platform of fixed array and sequencing technologies plus software solutions.
