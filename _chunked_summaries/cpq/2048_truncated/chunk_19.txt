### Victory Capital goes public with $8.6 billion rival bid for Janus Henderson | Reuters — 2026-02-26. Victory Capital (VCTR.O), based in San Antonio, went public on Thursday, February 26, 2026, with an $8.6 billion rival bid for Janus Henderson (JHG.N), ratcheting up a bidding war after Janus had already agreed in December 2025 to a $7.4 billion buyout by Nelson Peltz’s Trian and General Catalyst. Victory proposed a cash-and-stock mix of $30 in cash and 0.350 of its shares for each Janus share, valuing Janus at $57.04 per share; Reuters said the combined money manager would have more than $800 billion in assets and an enterprise value of $16 billion. Janus shares rose nearly 6% in afternoon trading while Victory’s stock fell more than 7%. Victory said it had submitted multiple bids in November and December that failed to draw any engagement from Janus’ board, and it argued its offer was “superior” to Trian’s, citing a premium consistent with recent industry deals such as Nuveen’s pending acquisition of Schroders. The Reuters report said the latest proposal valued Janus Henderson at 12.5 times 2026 earnings, compared with the 11 times Trian initially offered according to Evercore analysts, and that the proposal was fully financed through commitments from two investment banks. Victory still needed Janus’ independent board committee to bless the bid as a “superior proposal” before it could terminate the Trian agreement and negotiate, and Trian held about 20.7% of Janus stock according to LSEG. Janus said in December that it had agreed to the takeover after a five-year activist push by Peltz that began in 2020, and Janus itself was formed in 2017 via the merger of Henderson Group and Janus Capital; Reuters also noted the company had struggled with investor outflows and internal disputes since then. In a Business Wire/FT.com company announcement dated Feb. 26, 2026, Victory Capital Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: VCTR), headquartered in San Antonio, said it had sent another letter to the Special Committee of Janus Henderson Group plc’s board, again pushing a fully financed, actionable proposal to acquire Janus Henderson on terms it said provided meaningfully higher value than the deal with Trian Fund Management, L.P. Victory’s “best-of-both-worlds” proposal gave Janus shareholders total consideration of $57.04 per share, composed of $30.00 in cash plus a fixed exchange ratio of 0.350 shares of Victory Capital common stock based on Victory’s closing stock price as of February 25, 2026; Victory said that represented a 37% premium to Janus’ unaffected share price as of October 24, 2025 and an approximately 16% premium to the transaction contemplated with Trian. The letter said Janus shareholders would own approximately 38% of the combined company, which would have a total enterprise value of approximately $16 billion. Victory called the proposal a “Company Superior Proposal,” said it was fully financed with no financing conditions, and said it intended to retain substantially all Janus investment professionals, preserve the Janus Henderson brand, and minimize disruption for clients. Victory said it had a proven track record integrating firms, citing its acquisition of Pioneer as a recent example, and also referenced its 2019 acquisition of the asset management arm of USAA as evidence it could successfully integrate with Janus. Victory’s letter said it had submitted proposals on November 24, 2025, December 8, 2025, and December 22, 2025, but was denied meaningful engagement before Janus signed with Trian; it urged the Special Committee to fulfill fiduciary duties and engage. The proposal included no financing outs, full specific performance protection for Janus if financing failed, a lower client consent closing condition of 75% versus 80% in the Trian agreement, a lower 3% termination fee versus 4%, elimination of a $111.42 million expense-reimbursement obligation if shareholders reject the merger, and noted that if Janus terminated the Trian deal to accept Victory’s superior proposal, a $297.13 million termination fee would be due to Trian (4% of equity value). Victory said its pro forma gross leverage would be 3.5x 2025 EBITDA excluding synergies and 2.6x including synergies, versus 4.6x gross debt including preferred equity to 2025 EBITDA in the Trian transaction, and it estimated cost synergies of $500 million from middle- and back-office efficiencies, operational and administrative infrastructure, vendor consolidation and duplication, and scale. David C. Brown, Victory’s chairman and CEO, said the combination would create a more competitive platform with “superior value for shareholders, employees and clients alike,” while Victory’s public filing said the company had $323.2 billion in total client assets as of January 31, 2026 and cited TSR figures of over 2,000% since its 2013 MBO, approximately 600% since its IPO, more than 200% TSR outperformance versus Janus since Trian disclosed its Janus investment in October 2020, an 80% share-price increase after the Pioneer memorandum of understanding, and a 120% increase in the year after the USAA Asset Management announcement.
