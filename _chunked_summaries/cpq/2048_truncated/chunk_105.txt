Public Storage to Acquire National Storage Affiliates, Creating Significant Value for All Stakeholders – Company Announcement - FT.com, dated 2026-03-16, said Public Storage (NYSE: PSA) and National Storage Affiliates (NYSE: NSA) agreed to an all-stock acquisition valuing NSA at an enterprise value of approximately $10.5 billion, with NSA shareholders and NSA operating partnership (“OP”) unitholders receiving 0.14 of a share of PSA common stock or partnership units for each NSA share or unit, an implied offer price of $41.68 per NSA share based on PSA’s closing share price on March 13, 2026. The combined company is expected to have a pro forma equity market capitalization of approximately $57 billion and total enterprise value of approximately $77 billion. NSA’s portfolio was described as more than 1,000 properties, 69 million rentable square feet, and 550,000 units across 37 states and Puerto Rico, while Public Storage said it would wholly-own 488 of the over 1,000 properties on balance sheet, focusing on key Sun Belt and core markets, and that the transaction marks the first value creation milestone under its PS4.0™ plan. Both Boards of Trustees unanimously approved the deal, which is expected to close in the third quarter of 2026, subject to NSA equity holder approval and other customary conditions. Immediately before closing, PSA and NSA limited partners will form a joint venture consisting of 313 properties on NSA’s operating platform, totaling 19.6 million rentable square feet across 28 states and Puerto Rico, with an estimated value of approximately $3.3 billion; OP unitholders are expected to own about 80% of the JV at inception and PSA 20%, the JV is expected to be capitalized with $2.2 billion of property-level secured debt including a mezzanine loan investment from PSA of approximately $240 million, and it will operate at approximately 70% leverage. PSA will repay NSA’s bank debt and senior unsecured notes while assuming its mortgage debt and Series A, B and A-1 preferred shares and units, and it arranged $4.0 billion of committed financing from Goldman Sachs Bank USA and Wells Fargo Bank, National Association, split evenly between a $2.0 billion corporate bridge loan and a $2.0 billion JV off-balance sheet bridge loan.
