(NASDAQ: GTLB), the all-remote “intelligent orchestration platform for DevSecOps,” reported results for the fourth quarter fiscal year 2026, ended January 31, 2026, and for fiscal year 2026. CEO Bill Staples said “GitLab sits at the heart of how enterprises build and deliver software,” citing the launch of the GitLab Duo Agent Platform as bringing intelligent orchestration to the full software lifecycle; CFO Jessica Ross said fiscal year 2026 saw GitLab cross “$1 billion in ARR” and deliver “$220 million of free cash flow,” and that the board’s “new $400 million share repurchase authorization” reflects confidence in the business and commitment to shareholder value. Q4 FY 2026 revenue was $260.4 million, up 23% year over year from $211.4 million; GAAP operating margin was (2)% versus (9)% in Q4 FY 2025, non-GAAP operating margin 21% versus 18%, GAAP operating loss was $5.2 million, non-GAAP operating income $53.4 million, GAAP net loss attributable to GitLab was $2.6 million, non-GAAP net income attributable to GitLab $51.7 million, GAAP net loss per share diluted was $(0.02) versus $0.04, non-GAAP diluted net income per share was $0.30 versus $0.33, GAAP net cash provided by operating activities was $45.8 million, and non-GAAP adjusted free cash flow was $41.8 million versus $62.1 million a year earlier. Additional Q4 metrics included total RPO growth of 20% to $1.1 billion, cRPO growth of 24% to $719.4 million, Dollar-Based Net Retention Rate of 118%, customers with more than $1 million of ARR reaching 155 (+26% y/y), customers with more than $100,000 of ARR reaching 1,456 (+18%), and customers with more than $5,000 of ARR reaching 10,682 (+8%). Business highlights included appointing Jessica Ross as Chief Financial Officer and Siva Padisetty as Chief Technology Officer, introducing GitLab Credits as a usage-based pricing model for GitLab Duo Agent Platform capabilities that “provides transparency into AI costs” and lets customers scale agent usage aligned with business value, and announcing general availability of GitLab Duo Agent Platform for intelligent orchestration and agentic AI automation across the software lifecycle. Recent developments included the board authorizing an inaugural $400 million share repurchase program and GitLab Transcend on February 10, 2026, an exclusive event for technology leaders held both virtually and in person across 12 cities. Guidance for Q1 FY 2027 was revenue of $253.0 million to $255.0 million, non-GAAP operating income of $32.0 million to $34.0 million, and non-GAAP diluted net income per share of $0.20 to $0.21 assuming about 173 million weighted average shares; FY 2027 guidance was revenue of $1,099 million to $1,118 million, non-GAAP operating income of $129 million to $137 million, and non-GAAP diluted net income per share of $0.76 to $0.80 assuming about 175 million shares.
