Fed projects one rate cut this year as Powell vows to stay on until DOJ investigation is finished | AP News, 2026-03-18: the Federal Reserve on Wednesday kept its key interest rate unchanged after three cuts last year to 3.6%, with Chair Jerome Powell saying the outlook for the U.S. economy and inflation had become more uncertain because of the Iran war and that “nobody knows” how big the economic effects could be. Powell said the Fed would need to see further progress in goods prices declining as tariff effects fade before cutting again, and added the conditional line, “The rate forecast is conditional on the performance of the economy, so if we don’t see that progress then you won’t see the rate cut.” Markets reacted negatively, with the broad S&P 500 dropping 1.4%. In the Fed’s quarterly projections, officials modestly raised inflation forecasts, now seeing headline inflation at 2.7% by year-end and core inflation also at 2.7%, slightly below the 2.8% January reading; they slightly boosted growth and kept unemployment at 4.4%. Powell also said he had “no intention” of leaving the central bank before a DOJ investigation into his congressional testimony about the Fed’s building renovation is resolved, after a judge on the prior Friday threw out a pair of subpoenas the Justice Department had issued to the Fed and U.S. Attorney Jeannine Pirro said she would appeal. Powell’s term as Fed chair ends May 15, and President Donald Trump has nominated former Fed official Kevin Warsh as replacement, though confirmation has been delayed because key Republican senators oppose the probe; Powell said he had not yet decided whether to remain on the board until his governor term ends in January 2028. One governor, Stephen Miran, dissented for a quarter-point cut. The AP piece also noted inflation by the Fed’s preferred measure was 2.8% in January, up from 2.3% nearly a year earlier, core inflation was 3.1%, gas prices averaged $3.84 a gallon nationwide, up 92 cents from a month ago, February payrolls showed employers shed 92,000 jobs after January’s 130,000 gain, and the unemployment rate ticked up to 4.4%; Nathan Sheets of Citi said officials were aware they had missed their inflation target for five years and did not want to keep missing it, while Tim Duy of SGH Macro called the forecasts “stale,” and Powell cited the economy’s resilience through tariffs, the Fed’s 2022 and 2023 hikes, and the pandemic aftermath. ### Fed interest rate decision March 2026: Holds rates steady, 2026-03-18: Reuters-style coverage said the Federal Open Market Committee voted 11-1 to keep the benchmark federal funds rate in a range between 3.5%-3.75%, with Stephen Miran again dissenting for a quarter-point cut while Christopher Waller, who had joined Miran in wanting a cut in January, voted to hold.
