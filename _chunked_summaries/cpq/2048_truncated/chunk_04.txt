The ruling struck down 20-percent tariffs on China’s U.S.-bound exports imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act ("IEEPA") on national-emergency grounds tied to fentanyl distribution and trade imbalances, while other duties on Chinese goods under Section 301 and Section 232 remained in place; Trump said he would impose a new "10% global tariff" for "150 days" and later said he would raise the global tariff to "15%". Trump told visiting foreign leaders, "That's going to be a wild one," and "We have to put on the biggest display you've ever had in the history of China." The October 2025 South Korea meeting had followed Beijing’s promises to crack down on the illicit fentanyl trade and pause export restrictions on critical minerals; Taiwan remained central to later calls, with Xi raising U.S. arms sales to Taiwan, an island China claims as its own territory while Taipei rejects that claim, and the U.S. remaining bound by law to provide Taiwan with means to defend itself and having approved its largest-ever arms sale to Taiwan in December 2025, worth "$11.1 billion" in weapons. Xi also said on a February call he would consider increasing soybean purchases, a point important to struggling U.S. farmers, while analysts said the Supreme Court ruling could mean China is less affected than other countries, especially in Southeast Asia, because tariffs on China rest on more legally durable authorities; Scott Kennedy of the Center for Strategic and International Studies said Trump had been "playing defense" given Beijing’s rare earth leverage and that the defeat likely "cements his weakness in their eyes," and Martin Chorzempa of the Peterson Institute for International Economics said the ruling could indirectly increase pressure on Beijing if other countries’ effective tariff rates fall more than China’s. Trump’s global trade war, begun after he started his second term in January 2025, had alienated other trading partners, including allies, even as he had eased policy toward Beijing in areas from tariffs to advanced computer chips and drones and framed hawkish moves toward Canada, Greenland and Venezuela as aimed at thwarting China.
