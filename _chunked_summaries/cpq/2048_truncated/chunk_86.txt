### Gold slips on stronger dollar, higher rate expectations | 2026-03-09
Gold fell about 1% on Monday, with spot gold down 1.5% at $5,091.62 per ounce and U.S. gold futures for April delivery settling 1.1% lower at $5,103.70. The move came as a stronger dollar and expectations of higher interest rates pressured bullion, while the Middle East conflict fuelled inflation concerns. Jim Wyckoff, senior analyst at Kitco Metals, said inflation worries and higher rate expectations were weighing on gold, but a prolonged conflict could still sustain safe-haven demand and provide a floor for prices. The dollar firmed as oil prices neared $120 a barrel on fears that a protracted Middle East war could severely disrupt energy supplies and hurt global growth; a stronger dollar makes dollar-priced bullion more expensive for other currency holders. Israel’s military said it had launched attacks in central Iran and struck the Lebanese capital Beirut, and the war had effectively shut the Strait of Hormuz, through which a fifth of global oil and seaborne liquefied gas is shipped near Iran’s coast. The article said the U.S. consumer price index for February was due Wednesday and the Federal Reserve’s preferred inflation gauge, the PCE index, was due Friday, with the Fed’s next policy meeting scheduled for March 17–18 and widely expected to hold rates steady. It also listed spot silver down 0.2% at $84.18 per ounce, platinum up 1.1% to $2,158.02, and palladium up 2.4% to $1,663.79.
