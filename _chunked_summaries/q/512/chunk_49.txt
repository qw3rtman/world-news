Reuters | 2026-03-12 | Oil prices surge as Iran escalates tanker attacks, shares fall: Brent crude futures settled at $100.46 a barrel and U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude at $95.70, both the highest since August 2022; Brent touched a session high of $101.60, rose 9.2%, and WTI rose 9.7%. The surge followed Iran’s escalation of tanker attacks in the Gulf and the Strait of Hormuz, where at least 16 merchant ships had been struck since fighting began; Tehran warned of oil at $200 a barrel. Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Mojtaba Khamenei, in his first public statement after succeeding his slain father, said Iran would keep the Strait of Hormuz closed and attack U.S. bases “to avenge its martyrs,” while U.S. Energy Secretary Chris Wright said global oil prices were unlikely to hit $200 and U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said the U.S. Navy, perhaps with an international coalition, would escort vessels “when militarily possible.” Iraqi security officials said two fuel tankers were struck by explosive-laden Iranian boats in Iraqi waters and Iraqi oil ports had “completely stopped operations”; one crew member was killed in Basra and a container vessel was reported struck near the United Arab Emirates. Stocks fell, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average and S&P 500 down about 1.5% and the Nasdaq Composite off 1.8%; the STOXX 600 slipped 0.6% and the MSCI All-World index fell 1.5%. U.S. yields rose, with the 10-year Treasury up 5.5 basis points to 4.261% and two-year yields at a six-month high. Morgan Stanley fell 4%, Blackstone 4.7% and BlackRock 2.9% amid private-credit worries, as Partners Group warned the $2 trillion market could double. Reuters-pollled economists still expected the Fed to cut in June 2026, but nearly 40% saw one or no cuts this year. The euro slipped 0.45% to $1.1515, the dollar rose 0.28% versus the yen and more than 1.5% versus a basket, gold fell 1.7% to $5,088 an ounce, Monica Guerra warned persistent high oil could keep rates higher for longer, and Ayako Yoshioka said “the longer the conflict goes on, the more stress is inserted into global markets.” ### 11 Iranian Cluster Missiles Penetrated.
