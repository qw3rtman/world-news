Oil prices: Analysts raise the alarm as crude soars over Iran war, 2026-03-09: after fresh U.S. and Israeli strikes across Iran, including oil depots, Brent crude futures with May delivery rose 11.6% to trade at $103.47 per barrel and hit a session high of $119.5, while U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures with April delivery were 12.2% higher at $101.97 per barrel and hit a session high of $119.48. Analysts said the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz, through which typically about 20% of the world’s oil and gas passes, left shipping traffic all but halted; Iraq and Kuwait had already begun to shut in crude production. Neil Atkinson, former head of oil at the International Energy Agency, speaking on CNBC’s “Squawk Box Europe,” said there was no precedent for the closure and “the sky is the limit.” Janiv Shah, vice president of oil markets at Rystad Energy, said Brent could climb to $135 per barrel if the situation persisted for four months and that prices would stay above $110 on a two-month basis because “there is nowhere left to put the oil” as physical supply is choked off by drone strikes. Societe Generale said the UAE could be the next producer at risk within five to seven days, Qatar is vulnerable because its oil volumes are modest relative to LNG exposure, and Saudi Arabia could face shut-ins if the strait stays closed another two to three weeks; prolonged shut-ins would “materially increase” restart-complication risk. Tyler Goodspeed, ExxonMobil’s chief economist, said the old consensus assumed “abundant oil on the water” plus strategic reserves for short-term gaps, but now he thought more scenarios involved the strait remaining effectively closed longer; he said everyone but Russia had an interest in normal traffic resuming.
