On March 3, 2026, Israeli Defence Minister Israel Katz, together with Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, authorized the Israel Defense Forces to advance into Lebanon and take control of additional strategic positions to prevent attacks on Israeli border communities; military spokesman Brigadier General Effie Defrin said the Northern Command was creating a buffer zone between Israeli residents and threats by taking control of dominating terrain, while the army described the moves as “tactical measures” rather than a full-scale ground invasion and said “all options are on the table.” In response, the Lebanese army redeployed soldiers from border positions to their bases because of the danger to their safety, with eight to nine soldiers at each point, and a Lebanese government source said more than 58,000 people had been displaced by the ongoing Israeli strikes. Hezbollah, after an initial rocket attack on Israel on March 2, 2026, said it was avenging the killing of Iranian supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, and the Lebanese government declared an immediate ban on Hezbollah’s military activities. By Tuesday, March 3, 2026, Israel said it had struck more than 160 Hezbollah targets in south Lebanon, including the Radwan Force, and said it killed Hezbollah’s intelligence chief, Hussein Muakalled; Lieutenant General Eyal Zamir said Israel was determined to eliminate Hezbollah until it was disarmed. Israel warned residents to leave Tyre, a city with a UNESCO World Heritage site, and AFPTV reported series of air strikes hitting Beirut’s southern suburbs without prior warning, including command centres, weapons storage facilities, and satellite communication components. Hezbollah said it targeted three Israeli military bases in retaliation, and a rocket struck a house in the border village of Yuval, lightly injuring one person with glass shrapnel, according to Magen David Adom.
