### Iran’s supreme leader killed in major attack | AP News | 2026-02-28: In the joint U.S.-Israel airstrike on Saturday, February 28, 2026, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed; President Donald Trump justified the attack as necessary to disable Iran’s nuclear capabilities. The operation also killed Maj. Gen. Mohammad Pakpour, head of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, and Ali Shamkhani, a top security adviser to Khamenei. The U.S. military said the targets included Revolutionary Guard command facilities, air defense systems, missile and drone launch sites, and military airfields, while Israeli chief of staff Lt. Gen. Eyal Zamir said Air Force pilots struck “hundreds of targets.” Trump posted and spoke from Florida, urging Iranians to “take over your government” and calling Khamenei “one of the most evil people in History”; he also called on the Iranian Revolutionary Guard to lay down arms, offering immunity or “certain death.” Iranian state media confirmed Khamenei’s death early Sunday and declared “40 days” of public mourning plus a seven-day nationwide public holiday, with a black mourning flag raised at the Imam Reza shrine in Mashhad. Satellite photos from Airbus showed Khamenei’s compound in downtown Tehran heavily bombed, and the semiofficial Fars agency reported that relatives were also killed. The fighting spread regionally: Bahrain, Kuwait, and Qatar reported missile or drone impacts; Bahrain saw damage to three buildings in Manama and Muharraq, Kuwait said a drone hit the main international airport injuring employees and shrapnel hit Ali Al-Salem air base, Dubai saw air defense fire and one death from shrapnel, Jordan said it “dealt with” 49 drones and ballistic missiles, and Magen David Adom reported a woman died in the Tel Aviv area after being wounded. Iranian officials at the UN Security Council said hundreds of civilians were killed and wounded; Iranian authorities said at least 115 people were killed in southern Iran, including at a girls’ school, and AP noted Lamerd region governor Ali Alizadeh reported at least 15 killed in the southwest after strikes on a sports hall and residential areas. Capt. Tim Hawkins of U.S. Central Command said he was aware of reports about the girls’ school strike. President Trump said he had warned Iran with a “10-day” deadline, and a senior U.S. official said intelligence showed Iran had developed the capability to produce its own high-quality centrifuges despite Trump’s prior claim that the program had been “destroyed” in June 2025 strikes. Reuters and AP also noted no U.S. casualties and minimal damage at U.S. bases despite hundreds of Iranian missile and drone attacks; the U.S. said Gulf bases in Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Iraq, the UAE, and Jordan were hit.
