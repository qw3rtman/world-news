Nebius Names Dan Lawrence to Lead Expansion in the US as Senior Vice President and General Manager for the Americas | 2026-03-09: Nebius, the Nasdaq-listed AI cloud company (NASDAQ: NBIS), announced Dan Lawrence as Senior Vice President and General Manager for the Americas, based in the Boston area, to lead North American expansion and scale the go-to-market engine across “strategic, enterprise, ISV, and AI-native” customer segments. The Amsterdam-headquartered company said Lawrence will help drive expansion of purpose-built AI infrastructure in gigawatts across the United States, including gigawatt-scale data center campuses, and that Nebius had recently received approval for its first gigawatt-scale AI factory in the U.S.; it also plans a regional headquarters in San Francisco. Lawrence joins from Akamai Technologies, where he was Senior Vice President of Global Sales for Cloud and helped scale the compute business to approach $1 billion in revenue, and earlier held senior leadership roles at Amazon Web Services managing global enterprise engagements. Marc Boroditsky, Nebius Chief Revenue Officer, praised Lawrence’s track record of scaling cloud businesses to the billion-dollar mark; Lawrence said Nebius stood out for “the rare engineering DNA to design and operate the entire stack,” its “strong financial position” allowing capital and capacity deployment at unique scale, and “the pace of software innovation” being extraordinary. The release described Nebius as “one of the world’s largest independent AI cloud players,” serving startups and enterprises building AI products, agents, and services worldwide; it noted the company filed its Annual Report on Form 20-F for the year ended December 31, 2024, with the SEC on April 30, 2025, and included forward-looking statements under the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995, plus risk language about possible construction delays or complications in data center development.
