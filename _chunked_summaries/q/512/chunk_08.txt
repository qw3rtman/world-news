### 2026-02-24 | Stripe tender offer values company at $159 billion
Stripe’s February 24, 2026 tender offer valued the payments company at $159 billion, up from $107 billion last year, with Axios Pro first breaking the news in early February when investors were talking about a valuation of over $140 billion. The deal was said to include investors Thrive, Coatue, and a16z, and Stripe itself provided the majority of the shares for sale by buying back stock, a sign the company is in no rush to IPO. In its annual letter for the period ending in 2025, Stripe said it processed $1.9 trillion in payments volume in 2025, up 34% from the prior year, with President John Collison attributing the growth to rising enterprise usage, citing Microsoft, Nvidia, and Amazon; he said Microsoft had shifted a “significant fraction” of its payment volumes to Stripe. Stripe also said the 2025 startup cohort was “the strongest, biggest and fastest cohort” it had ever seen and “50% faster than the prior cohorts.” The company did not disclose revenue, but said it “remains robustly profitable,” and the analysis noted payments volume is only an imperfect proxy for revenue. Stripe has been trying to diversify beyond core payments because payments are “an often lumpy source of revenue”; its fastest-growing segment by total dollars was the revenue suite, handling billing, tax, and invoicing automation, expected to hit a $1 billion annual run rate.
