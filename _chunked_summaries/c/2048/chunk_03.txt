Gilead Sciences to Acquire Arcellx to Maximize Long-Term Potential of Anito-cel – Company Announcement - FT.com, Feb. 23, 2026 (published as Feb 23 2026 11:01 GMT), said Gilead Sciences, Inc. (Nasdaq: GILD) entered a definitive agreement to acquire Arcellx (Nasdaq: ACLX) for $115 per share in cash at closing plus one contingent value right of $5 per share, implying an equity value of $7.8 billion, with the transaction approved by both boards and expected to close in the second quarter of 2026 subject to customary conditions, including tender of enough shares to reach a majority and regulatory approvals; Gilead already owned approximately 11.5 percent of Arcellx’s outstanding common stock. The deal centers on Arcellx’s anitocabtagene autoleucel, “anito-cel,” a BCMA-directed CAR T-cell therapy for patients with multiple myeloma, building on a 2022 Kite-Arcellx collaboration and on the FDA acceptance of the BLA for treatment of adult patients with relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma, with an anticipated PDUFA date of December 23, 2026. The press release said clinical studies showed “deep and durable responses” with a “predictable and manageable safety profile,” and that the BLA for a fourth-line indication was supported by Phase 1 (NCT04155749) and pivotal Phase 2 iMMagine1 (NCT05396885) data. Under the CVR, holders get an additional $5 per CVR if cumulative global net sales of anito-cel reach at least $6.0 billion from launch through year-end 2029. Daniel O’Day said the move reflects “our conviction in the potential of anito-cel” and could make it a “foundational treatment” including earlier lines of therapy, while Rami Elghandour said Kite is “well-positioned to maximize access to anito-cel.” Gilead said the acquisition gives it full control of anito-cel, accelerating development and commercialization while eliminating profit-share, milestones, and royalties; the company said the transaction could be accretive to EPS in 2028 and thereafter.
