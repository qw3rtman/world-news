### Gilead Sciences to Acquire Arcellx to Maximize Long-Term Potential of Anito-cel – Company Announcement - FT.com, Feb. 23, 2026: Gilead Sciences Inc. and Arcellx Inc. announced a definitive agreement under which Gilead will acquire Arcellx for $115 per share in cash at closing plus one contingent value right worth $5 per share, implying an equity value of $7.8 billion. Gilead said the deal gives it full control of anitocabtagene autoleucel, or anito-cel, a BCMA-directed CAR T-cell therapy for multiple myeloma, eliminating profit-share, milestones, and royalties from the companies’ existing Kite/Arcellx collaboration begun in 2022. The FDA has already accepted anito-cel’s BLA for adult patients with relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma; the application, supported by the Phase 1 study NCT04155749 and pivotal Phase 2 iMMagine1 study NCT05396885, has an anticipated PDUFA date of December 23, 2026 and is positioned as a fourth-line treatment. Gilead said the transaction, approved by both boards and expected to close in the second quarter of 2026 subject to customary conditions and the tender of shares representing a majority of Arcellx’s outstanding stock, would let it accelerate development and commercialization; Gilead already owns about 11.5 percent of Arcellx’s common stock. The offer values Arcellx at a 68 percent premium to its 30-day volume-weighted average share price as of February 20, 2026, and includes a $5 CVR payable if cumulative global net sales of anito-cel reach at least $6.0 billion from launch through year-end 2029. Daniel O’Day said the deal reflects conviction that anito-cel could become “a foundational treatment” for multiple myeloma, including earlier lines, while Arcellx CEO Rami Elghandour praised Gilead and said Kite is well positioned to maximize access.
