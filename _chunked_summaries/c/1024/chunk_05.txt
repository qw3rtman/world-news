### 2026-02-24, “Here’s who members of Congress from Minnesota brought to Trump’s State of the Union - CBS Minnesota” / “Here’s who members of Congress from Minnesota are bringing to Trump’s State of the Union - CBS Minnesota”
Residents detained by federal agents and the son of a slain state lawmaker were among the guests the Minnesota delegation brought to President Trump’s State of the Union address on Tuesday night in Washington, D.C.; the speech was Mr. Trump’s first State of the Union address of his second term, after he had addressed a joint session of Congress last March that was not a formal State of the Union. Among the Minnesota guests, Colin Hortman, son of Minnesota House Speaker Emerita Melissa Hortman, attended with Rep. Kelly Morrison, and Rep. Betty McCollum brought Colin’s wife, Alina Bachman Hortman. Melissa and Mark Hortman were shot and killed in their Brooklyn Park home last June in what prosecutors called a politically motivated attack, and after their deaths Colin and Sophie Hortman said their parents “touched so many lives” and “leave behind an incredible legacy of dedication to their community that will live on in us.” Rep. Ilhan Omar brought four guests meant to reflect how ICE’s actions in Minnesota through “Operation Metro Surge” had hurt “safety and economic security”: Aliya Rahman, who was dragged from her car on the way to a doctor’s appointment and detained by federal agents, said on video she had a disability and later testified she can no longer lift her arms normally and described “horrific” conditions at the Whipple Federal Building in Minneapolis; Mary Granlund, chair of the Columbia Heights Public School Board, whose district drew attention after 5-year-old Liam Ramos and his father were detained outside their home near Valley View Elementary School; Mubashir Hussen, a 20-year-old U.S. citizen detained violently during lunch in Cedar-Riverside and suing over alleged racial profiling and warrantless arrests; and Gerardo Orozco Guzman, whose father was detained in early January and is held at Cibola County Correctional Center in New Mexico. Rep. Pete Stauber brought conservative YouTuber Nick Shirley; Shirley and David Hoch had made a December viral video accusing, often without evidence, a dozen Somali-owned day care centers of fraud, after which the Trump administration sent as many as 3,000 federal agents to Minnesota to crack down on fraud and undocumented immigration, though federal officials later gave no updates after border czar Tom Homan said the surge was winding down. Sen. Tina Smith boycotted the address and instead attended the National Mall’s “People’s State of the Union” with Fridley Public Schools Superintendent Brenda Lewis, whom Smith praised as “an incredible example” of Minnesotans organizing “in the face of a violent federal police force”; Lewis said her district had endured “the unacceptable reality” at the hands of ICE, and Smith said, “Families are torn apart and they will never be put back together again. The pain and the suffering and the damage is going to last for a very, very long time and this is what Donald Trump and Kristi Noem are doing to us right now.” Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey also appeared in Washington at a different event, “State of the Swamp,” where he said Minneapolis showed “the forces of terror and division can never conquer a city that loves its neighbors more than it fears retribution,” and Virginia Gov. Abigail Spanberger, elected in November as the state’s first female governor, delivered the Democratic response.
