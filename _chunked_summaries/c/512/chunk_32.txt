### 2026-03-04 | Texas Senate primary runoff ahead for Cornyn, Paxton as Talarico beats Crockett ### In Texas, the marquee races in Tuesday’s primaries were the two competitive Senate contests. Republican Sen. John Cornyn and Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton failed to reach the GOP nomination threshold and will advance to a May 26 runoff, while state Rep. James Talarico locked down the Democratic nomination by defeating Rep. Jasmine Crockett, NBC News projected early on March 4, 2026. The race drew almost $100 million in advertising; NBC News cited AdImpact showing Cornyn and allies spent a combined $78.5 million on ads versus $4.4 million for Paxton and allied outside groups. Trump had not endorsed in the GOP primary, though a White House source said an endorsement during the runoff period was likely. Cornyn warned Paxton would be “dead weight to the top of the ticket,” while Paxton told supporters, “Texas is not for sale,” and said Cornyn spent “around 100 million” trying to “buy this seat.” Voting was disrupted in Dallas and Williamson counties after confusion over precinct-based voting; the Texas Supreme Court blocked lower-court orders to keep Democratic polling sites open past “7 p.m. local time,” ordering ballots cast by voters not in line before then to be separated. Talarico, speaking at midnight in Austin, said, “Every vote must be counted. Every voice must be heard,” and later, “We are still waiting for an official call, but we are confident in this movement we built together.” Crockett said, “I’m not gonna have election results tonight in my opinion,” blamed Republicans for targeting Dallas County, and later conceded after the call. The Democratic race turned on turnout and style: Talarico, a 36-year-old Presbyterian seminarian and former teacher from Round Rock, argued for a “politics of love” and broad appeal, while Crockett, 44, a Dallas-area firebrand, emphasized fighting Trump and base mobilization.
