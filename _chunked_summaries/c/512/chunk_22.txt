Balen Shah: Rapper, mayor, Nepal’s next prime minister? | Elections | Al Jazeera | 2026-03-02 — Kathmandu, Nepal, the 35-year-old Balendra Shah (“Balen”), former Kathmandu mayor and now parliamentary candidate of the Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP), is presented as a possible future prime minister after Nepal’s September 2025 Gen Z protests toppled then-Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli. At a rally in Lalitpur on February 28, 2026, he told supporters, “I love you.” Born in 1990 in Kathmandu, youngest of four siblings, son of ayurveda practitioner Ram Narayan Shah (who died in December), he studied civil engineering at Himalayan Whitehouse International College and earned a postgraduate degree in structural engineering from Visvesvaraya Technological University in Karnataka, India. He broke out via the 2013 Raw Barz rap battle, then won Kathmandu’s mayoralty in May 2022 as an independent with 61,767 votes, defeating Nepali Congress (38,341) and UML (38,117). After the September 2025 unrest left dozens dead and exposed youth anger, he backed protesters, then in November posted “F*** America, F*** India, F*** China, F*** UML, F*** Congress, F*** RSP, F*** RPP, F*** Maobaadi. You Guys all Combined can do nothing,” deleting it within half an hour. In January he quit as mayor and joined the RSP, then refused Oli’s proposed prime-minister debate, demanding accountability for the civilian deaths and calling Oli a “terrorist.” He is running in Jhapa-5, about 300km southeast of Kathmandu, against Oli in the March 5 election, despite Jhapa being an Oli/CPN-UML stronghold. The piece notes his all-black clothing, black rectangular sunglasses, 3.5 million Facebook followers, 1 million on Instagram, 400,000 on X and nearly 1 million on YouTube, plus his songs “Nepal Haseko” (over 10 million YouTube views; chorus: “I want to see Nepal smiling; I want to see Nepalis living happily.”) and “Balidan” (14 million views); on Discord’s “Youth Against Corruption,” “Balen” was mentioned 16,328 times.
