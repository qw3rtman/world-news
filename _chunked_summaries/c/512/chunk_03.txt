Gilead Sciences to Acquire Arcellx to Maximize Long-Term Potential of Anito-cel – Company Announcement - FT.com, Feb. 23, 2026 11:01 GMT: Gilead Sciences, Inc. (Nasdaq: GILD) said in Foster City, Calif. & Redwood City, Calif., that it entered a definitive agreement to acquire Arcellx (Nasdaq: ACLX) for "$115 per share in cash at closing and one contingent value right of $5 per share," implying "$7.8 billion" equity value. The deal builds on Gilead’s 2022 collaboration with Arcellx on anitocabtagene autoleucel (anito-cel), an investigational BCMA-directed CAR T-cell therapy for multiple myeloma; Kite already co-develops and co-commercializes it. Arcellx’s BLA for fourth-line relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma was accepted by the FDA, with an anticipated PDUFA date of December 23, 2026; support came from Phase 1 NCT04155749 and pivotal Phase 2 iMMagine1 NCT05396885. Terms include a 68 percent premium to Arcellx’s 30-day VWAP as of February 20, 2026, a CVR paying an extra "$5 per CVR" if cumulative global net sales of anito-cel reach "$6.0 billion" from launch through year-end 2029, and closing expected in Q2 2026 subject to tendering enough shares plus approvals; Gilead already owned about 11.5 percent.
