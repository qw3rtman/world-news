11 Iranian Cluster Missiles Penetrated. One Dropped 70 Bombs Over Central Israel - National Security & Cyber, March 12, 2026: on Thursday night at 22:59, with cellphones in central Israel warning of Iranian missile fire, sirens seven minutes later, and videos showing dozens of bright points of light over the greater Tel Aviv area, Haaretz reported that a cluster missile—likely a Khorramshahr ballistic missile with a warhead dispersing up to 80 bomblets—penetrated Israel’s air defenses and spread bomblets across a 27-kilometer stretch from Peduel in the West Bank to Holon on the Mediterranean coast; the missile contained at least 70 bomblets per Jordanian footage, and though its bomblets caused damage, no one was injured in that strike. The paper said 11 cluster missiles had penetrated Israel since the war began, compared with only three in last year’s war with Iran, and that Iran has launched about 250 missiles in the current war, roughly half cluster missiles. The article said the first cluster missile of the war arrived on February 28, struck at least five sites including Ramat Gan, and left a crater, a basement ceiling penetrated to 15 centimeters, and damage to a 90-year-old concrete-block building; this week two construction workers in Yehud were killed after remaining outside shelters, and two others in Tel Aviv and Or Yehuda were seriously injured. It quoted Tal Inbar of the Missile Defense Advocacy Alliance on bomblets intended for airfields, soft vehicles, radar stations, and other area targets, and Chief Superintendent Doron Lavi saying cluster munitions create “many small explosions,” “greater panic,” and unexploded ordnance that can remain for months or years.
