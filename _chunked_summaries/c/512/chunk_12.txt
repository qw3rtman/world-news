Here’s how world leaders are reacting to operation 'Epic Fury' : NPR | 2026-02-28: world leaders reacted cautiously after the United States and Israel attacked Iran amid weeks of escalating tensions and a major U.S. military buildup in the region. U.N. Secretary General António Guterres condemned the “use of force” by the U.S. and Israel and the retaliatory Iranian response, saying both “undermine international peace and security,” and called for an immediate cessation of hostilities and de-escalation. The U.N. Security Council held an emergency meeting Saturday afternoon, chaired by Britain. European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen called the developments “greatly concerning” and urged restraint, nuclear safety, and respect for international law; EU foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas called the situation “perilous,” stressed civilian protection, and also said the Iranian regime “has killed thousands” and its support for terror groups “pose a serious threat to global security.” The leaders of France, Germany and the U.K. (the E3) issued a joint statement saying they had long urged Iran to end its nuclear program, stop destabilizing actions, and “cease the appalling violence and repression against its own people”; they said none of their countries participated in the strikes, stayed in close contact with the U.S., Israel and others, and condemned Iranian retaliation, saying “Iran must refrain from indiscriminate military strikes.” Spain’s Pedro Sánchez rejected the “unilateral military action by the United States and Israel,” urged “immediate de-escalation” and a return to dialogue, while Canada’s Mark Carney said Canada supports the U.S. “acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon” and called Iran “the principal source of instability and terror throughout the Middle East.” Russia called the strikes “a pre-planned and unprovoked act” of aggression and warned of “uncontrolled escalation”; China said it was “highly concerned,” demanded respect for Iran’s sovereignty and an immediate stop to military actions; Ukraine’s Volodymyr Zelenskyy endorsed the U.S. acting “decisively,” citing Iran’s role arming Russia with Shahed drones used more than 57,000 times against Ukrainian cities. Oman’s mediator Badr Albusaidi, who had met Vice President Vance in Washington Friday and said Iran agreed to “zero stockpiling of nuclear enrichment,” was “dismayed” and told the U.S. “this is not your war”; Lebanon’s Nawaf Salam warned Lebanon would not accept being dragged into “adventures” threatening its security.
