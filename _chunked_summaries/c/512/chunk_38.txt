Unexpected job losses, rise in unemployment rate fan US labor market doubts | Reuters | 2026-03-06: In WASHINGTON, March 6, Reuters reported the U.S. economy unexpectedly lost jobs in February and unemployment rose to 4.4%, heightening doubts about labor-market stability and complicating the Federal Reserve’s stance amid rising oil prices. The Labor Department’s Bureau of Labor Statistics said nonfarm payrolls fell by 92,000 jobs in February after a downwardly revised 126,000 increase in January, versus Reuters-polled economists’ forecast of a 59,000 gain; estimates ranged from a loss of 9,000 to a gain of 125,000. It was the sixth monthly payroll decline since January 2025 and the second largest. The drop reflected, in part, a strike by 31,000 Kaiser Permanente healthcare workers, a winter storm, and payback after January’s model-flattered surge from the “birth-and-death model.” Healthcare shed 28,000 jobs, physicians’ offices 37,000, information 11,000, federal government 10,000, transportation and warehousing 11,000, construction 11,000, leisure and hospitality 27,000, and manufacturing 12,000; average hourly earnings rose 0.4% for a 3.8% annual gain. The labor-force participation rate fell to 62.0%, the lowest since December 2021, after January’s rate was revised to 62.1%; household employment dropped 185,000 and the labor force and employment were revised down by about 1.4 million from January population-control changes. Economists said gains averaged just 6,000 a month in the three months through February, down from 50,000 in the prior three months. Miran argued high inflation readings reflect measurement issues, citing portfolio-management fees, and said rising oil prices tied to the Iran war were less concerning because energy shocks are usually one-off; he said he hopes the Fed votes to cut. He has dissented at each FOMC meeting he has attended since September, preferring half-point cuts in 2025 and a quarter-point cut in January when the committee held rates steady.
