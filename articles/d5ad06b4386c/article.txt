Donald J. Trump made a joke about the Pearl Harbor attack when he met with Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe during a state visit to Japan in November 2017, Trump's own White House records show.

The conversation, cited in White House logs obtained by The Washington Post, took place as a guest of honor mentioned the surprise attack on the U.S. naval base in 1941.

"Guest mentioned Pearl Harbor. P resident joked about it being 75 years ago," the Presidential Daily Briefing for Nov. 7, 2017, states.

The comment resurfaced ahead of Trump's meeting this week in Florida with Japanese Foreign Minister ToshimitsuMotegi. Trump has previously drawn rebukes from allies and foreign leaders for making what they've called inappropriate or offensive remarks about the war.
