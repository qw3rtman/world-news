# Trump lashes out by condemning Starmer as ‘no Churchill’

The president expressed annoyance over the UK’s reluctance to let US jets use the island of Diego Garcia to launch strikes on Iran

Tuesday March 03 2026, 9.35pm, The Times

Sir Keir Starmer is no Winston Churchill, President Trump has said, as he suggested that the prime minister had ruined the special relationship over the use of British bases to attack Iran.

The US president said he was “not happy” with the UK and “this is not Winston Churchill that we’re dealing with” after Starmer took days to make up his mind about whether US jets could carry out strikes from the “stupid island” of Diego Garcia.

Trump’s latest, unprompted, attack from the Oval Office came as No 10 faced criticism for waiting until Tuesday afternoon to give the go-ahead to send a Royal Navy warship to protect Cyprus after RAF Akrotiri was hit by a drone.

HMS Dragon, a Type-45 destroyer capable of shooting down ballistic missiles and drones, will head to the region with two Wildcat helicopters on board amid concerns troops are vulnerable to attack.

HMS Dragon, a Type-45 destroyer, was built for anti-aircraft and anti-missile warfare

However, it will not arrive for at least eight days, it is understood, because its crew are at three days’ notice to move. The UK has no Royal Navy ships in the Middle East for the first time in half a century.

Greece also sent four F-16 fighter jets and two frigates to Cyprus.

Trump has answered his first questions from reporters since the US and Israel launched strikes against Iran on Saturday. Iran has responded by firing missiles and drones at neighbouring Arab states and strangling shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, a key waterway for energy trade.

## In other developments:

Last night, an Iranian missile hit an American air base, al-Udeid, in Qatar, although there were no reports of casualties.

Iranian drones also hit the US embassy in Riyadh, the capital of Saudi Arabia, and near the US consulate in Dubai as the US moved to evacuate its citizens from the region.

Israeli forces allegedly struck a meeting of Iran’s Supreme Council as officials gathered to choose a successor to the late Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.

The conflict spread deeper into Lebanon as the Israeli military hit Beirut with more airstrikes and deployed troops in the south.

Trump said the US navy would escort oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz if “necessary” and ordered Washington to provide insurance for shipping in the Gulf.

An RAF F-35B jet operating over Jordanian airspace shot down a drone, marking the first time it has destroyed a target on operations, while British troops took out multiple drones in Iraqi airspace that were heading towards coalition forces.

US Central Command said the military had struck more than 1,700 targets since Operation Epic Fury began.

Iran’s defence ministry said it was ready for a long war and had yet to use its most advanced weapons.

The European Union’s asylum agency warned the conflict in Iran could induce a migration influx of “unprecedented magnitude”.

Thousands of Kurdish militants backed by the US and Israel are expected to begin a ground operation in western Iran within days, ITV reported.

Trump boasted of wide damage inflicted on Iran by the attack, saying: “Just about everything’s been knocked out.”

The president’s remarks were at odds with a British government assessment that showed no sign of “any major slowing or any major reduction” in Iran’s capability to deter the US-Israeli operation.

Advertisement

Trump went on to attack Starmer for blocking the American use of Diego Garcia for its initial strikes on Iran because they were deemed illegal.

He said: “That island … It’s taken three, four days for us to work out where we can land there. It would have been much more convenient landing there as opposed to flying many extra hours so we are very surprised. This is not Winston Churchill that we’re dealing with.

“I will say the UK has been very, very uncooperative with that stupid island that they have, that they gave away and took a 100-year lease; having to do with, perhaps, indigenous people claiming the island that never even saw the island before. What’s that all about? They’ve ruined relationships.”

Trump claimed he loved the UK because his mother was born there but again criticised Britain’s approach to energy and migration.

“The UK, what they’re doing with energy and what they’re doing with immigration is horrible,” he said. “They have got windmills all over the place that are ruining the country, ruining the landscapes, ruining the beautiful fields. Open up the North Sea.”

Advertisement

The Trump administration has given conflicting reasons for going to war. The secretary of state, Marco Rubio, said on Monday that the US did so only after learning that its ally Israel was going to strike. Trump contradicted that, saying he had acted to prevent Tehran launching an assault first.

Starmer announced on Tuesday that the UK would send helicopters with counter-drone capabilities and HMS Dragon to protect British military personnel in Cyprus. The two Wildcat helicopters will be equipped with Martlet missiles to shoot down Iranian drones.

Starmer wrote on X: “The UK is fully committed to the security of Cyprus and British military personnel based there. We will always act in the interest of the UK and our allies.”

France will dispatch the aircraft carrier Charles de Gaulle to the Mediterranean. It will also send a frigate as well as anti-missile and anti-drone systems to Cyprus, President Macron confirmed on Tuesday night.

Service families were evacuated from RAF Akrotiri after a drone hit on Sunday

ALEXIS MITAS/GETTY IMAGES

Macron said Iran “bears primary responsibility” for the conflict, but added: “The United States of America and Israel have decided to launch military operations; they were conducted outside international law, which we cannot approve.”

Advertisement

Questions have been raised about why the Ministry of Defence did not send a navy warship to the region earlier, given it is the only capability the UK has for shooting down ballistic missiles.

John Foreman, former defence attaché to Moscow and Kyiv, said: “It doesn’t take a genius to work out that the US were building up to something huge. Prudent planning would have involved ensuring Cyprus is safe by deploying a destroyer, and also Royal Artillery and RAF regiment while dispersing civilians and aircraft.”

James Cartlidge, the shadow defence secretary, said: “It is extraordinary that at a time of such threats to our people, allies and interests in the Middle East that we haven’t a single Royal Navy warship in the region.

“Starmer may now belatedly be sending a destroyer, which should have happened days ago, but what we also needed to see was a serious plan to get to 3 per cent on defence spending.”

The RAF was criticised for using air-to-air missiles, costing between £200,000 and £2 million each, to shoot down cheap Iranian suicide drones, which cost £26,000 each.

Advertisement

Military analysts compared the tactic to “firing golden bullets at plastic targets”, saying Britain has failed to learn lessons from Ukrainian forces and should instead use heavy machineguns or anti-aircraft artillery.

When asked about the worst-case scenario, Trump said it would be “we do this, and then somebody takes over who’s as bad as the previous person, right? That could happen. We don’t want that to happen … You go through this, and then in five years you realise you put somebody in who’s no better. So we’d like to see somebody in there that’s going to bring it back for the people.”

He vowed to “cut off all dealings” with Spain for not allowing use of its bases. “Spain has been terrible … we’re going to cut off all trade,” he said.

In a post on Truth Social, Trump reversed his position from Sunday that he had agreed to talk to Iran, saying: “They want to talk. I said ‘Too Late!’ ”
