# Where the DHS shutdown could start to hurt

## So far the budget impasse has caused little public outcry. That may soon change

Share

Photograph: Getty Images

Feb 23rd 2026|WASHINGTON, DC|4 min read

Donald Trump will give his state-of-the-union speech on February 24th in unusual circumstances. America is amassing a huge military force in the Middle East, as the president contemplates an attack on Iran. The Supreme Court, whose justices will be seated in front of Mr Trump, has just struck down his signature policy on tariffs. And the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), which oversees everything from airport security to drug busts, has been shut down since February 14th, after lawmakers failed to agree on a funding deal. It will be the first state-of-the-union address delivered with part of the government closed.
