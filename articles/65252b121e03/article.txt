Crude Will Top $90 a Barrel Due to Straits of Hormuz Disruption, Say Analysts

By

George Glover

Oil prices are likely to top $90 a barrel due to the geopolitical crisis in the Middle East, TD Cowen said in a research note published on Sunday.

Analysts wrote that the Straits of Hormuz, a key oil-shipping route through which 20% of the world’s oil passes, was "effectively closed" due to the ongoing conflict, with increased insurance costs and associated risks slowing flows to "a trickle."

The analysts said that traders were likely to apply a risk premium of $15 to $20 a barrel because of the conflict.
