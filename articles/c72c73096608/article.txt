After Israel's ultimatum, Lebanon orders arrest of Iran IRGC members

By becoming an anchor of resistance in Lebanon, Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) has recently drawn the ire of the Lebanese government, especially in the wake of Israel’s military threats.

On the back of Israel’s sustained military campaign in southern Lebanon and the ongoing war in Gaza, Lebanon has ordered the arrest of Iran's IRGC members to curb their influence and activities in the country. The move is part of a broader push to limit foreign interference, particularly from Iran, which has long seen Lebanon as a conduit for its regional ambitions.

The Lebanese General Security Directorate (GSD) announced on March 26 that it is pursuing Iran's IRGC members, citing the need to protect national security and sovereignty. This action follows Israel's explicit military ultimatum demanding that Lebanon limit Iranian operations in its territory.

"The Lebanese government has been working to uphold its independence by limiting the influence of foreign military and paramilitary groups,” a GSD statement said. “This includes taking all legal measures against Iran’s IRGC presence in Lebanon, which operates in defiance of the country's laws."

Iran has accused Lebanon of unjustly cracking down on its operatives, even as it organizes funerals and military parades for the IRGC in the country. Lebanon’s relationship with Iran has long been complicated, given the significant political and military influence Iran wields through the militant group Hezbollah. Yet lately, there has been a visible shift in Lebanon’s stance, even as Hezbollah remains a powerful actor in the country.

The repressive steps against IRGC operatives have also raised concerns about the potential fallout with Iran. Analysts say the move could further inflame regional tensions, particularly if Tehran views the action as a direct threat to its strategic interests in Lebanon.

Meanwhile, diplomatic discussions between Lebanon and Iran are underway, led by Hezbollah’s political wing, which seeks to de-escalate the situation amid fears of a broader regional clash. Some believe the ongoing ceasefire talks between Israel and Lebanon may provide a window for de-escalation.

However, with both Israel and Lebanon taking increasingly firm positions against Iran's military presence, the prospect of a renewed conflict in the Middle East looms large.
