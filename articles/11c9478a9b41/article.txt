India GDP Growth Live: Why has base year been revised after so long?

#### Under normal circumstances, the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation has aimed to update the base year at five-year intervals in line with international guidelines.The base year for the national accounts is now being shifted from 2011–12 to 2022–23. Although the ministry has sought to follow a five-year revision cycle, the period between FY 2017–18 and FY 2021–22 was considered unsuitable for selecting a new base year.FY 2017–18 saw the introduction of the Goods and Services Tax, a major structural reform that required time for the system to stabilise. Meanwhile, FY 2019–20 and FY 2020–21 were heavily affected by the Covid-19 pandemic.FY 2021–22 also could not be adopted as the base year because GDP growth during that period was unusually high, largely reflecting the base effect associated with the post-pandemic recovery.

Gold Rate Today Live: Valuation methodology for gold, silver held by MFs revised

#### Capital markets regulator Sebi on Thursday introduced changes to the method used by mutual fund schemes to determine the value of physical gold and silver holdings, requiring the adoption of polled spot prices released by recognised stock exchanges.Under the revised framework, the spot prices used to settle physically delivered bullion derivative contracts will serve as the reference for valuing such assets. This replaces the earlier system that relied on benchmark-linked pricing.According to a Sebi circular, the new rules will come into force from April 1, 2026. “It has been decided that with effect from April 01, 2026...the mutual funds shall value physical gold and silver by using the polled spot prices published by the recognised stock exchanges which are used for settlement of physically delivered gold and silver derivatives contracts,” Sebi stated.At present, exchange traded funds investing in gold and silver determine the value of their holdings using the London Bullion Market Association’s AM fixing prices. These international benchmarks are then adjusted for exchange rates, freight expenses, import duties, taxes and other charges to derive domestic prices.The revised approach, introduced in line with the Sebi (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026, is intended to align valuations more closely with local market realities while improving consistency and transparency.The Association of Mutual Funds in India, working together with Sebi, will lay down a standardised framework for implementing the new valuation system.

Gold Rate Today Live: Gold can enhance portfolio returns without significantly raising risk, says WhiteOak Capital MF

#### Gold is widely regarded as a safe-haven asset, but a study by WhiteOak Capital Mutual Fund suggests it can also enhance portfolio returns when combined with equity and debt.Analysing data from September 2001 to January 2026, the study found that a pure debt portfolio delivered average returns of 6.87% with volatility of 6.40%. However, adding gold and equity — such as a mix of 55% debt, 25% equity and 20% gold — lifted average returns to 11.55%, while volatility remained largely similar at 6.85%.The study highlighted that gold, equity and debt often move differently across market cycles, helping balance portfolio performance. It also noted that modest equity allocations did not necessarily increase risk. For instance, a 90% bond–10% equity portfolio generated higher average returns of 8.09% with lower volatility than a pure debt portfolio.WhiteOak Capital said that since consistently timing asset classes is difficult, a diversified mix may help investors achieve better risk-adjusted returns. While past trends do not ensure future outcomes, the study underscored gold’s role in building more stable long-term portfolios.
