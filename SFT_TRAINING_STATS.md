## Batch-size guidance
For full-param SFT at sequence length `L`, the per-step token budget is `batch_size × L`. To capture the full distribution without truncation, set `L = max(effective_seq_len)` for the corpus you're training on:

- **world_news_C  (NTP — article-only NTP loss)**: `L_p99 = 1484`, `L_max = 1585`
- **world_news_Q  (SFT — synthetic Q→A pairs)**: `L_p99 = 93`, `L_max = 233`
- **world_news_C+Q  (interleaved corpus + Q→A)**: `L_p99 = 1176`, `L_max = 1585`

If you train with `group_by_length=True` or sequence packing, mean throughput is closer to `mean(effective_seq_len)` per row.

## Notes
- **NTP-C**: `question` is empty — training is plain next-token-prediction over the article body in `answer`. No chat template is applied.
- **SFT-Q**: synthetic Q→A pairs from `train-simple-qa`. Chat template applied; loss computed only on `answer` tokens.
- **C+Q**: interleaves `corpus` rows (article-only, NTP-style) and `qa` rows (Q→A, SFT-style). Corpus rows skip chat templating; QA rows get it. Ordering: each article's corpus row precedes its derived qa rows; articles ordered by `published_at`.
